# Strategic Analysis Process

## Learning Objectives

- Apply structured problem-solving to strategic questions
- Use the MECE principle to decompose problems
- Develop and test strategic hypotheses
- Prioritize analyses using 80/20 thinking
- Synthesize findings into actionable insights

---

## Introduction

Consultants at McKinsey, BCG, and Bain follow disciplined processes to solve complex strategic problems. The secret isn't genius—it's methodology. By structuring problems systematically, generating hypotheses, and focusing on what matters most, strategists can deliver insights in weeks that might otherwise take months. This chapter teaches the core analytical process.

---

## The Structured Problem-Solving Process

### Overview

```
SEVEN-STEP PROCESS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. DEFINE the problem                             │
│    ↓                                               │
│ 2. STRUCTURE the analysis (MECE decomposition)    │
│    ↓                                               │
│ 3. HYPOTHESIZE (develop initial answer)           │
│    ↓                                               │
│ 4. PRIORITIZE (80/20 focus)                       │
│    ↓                                               │
│ 5. GATHER data and evidence                       │
│    ↓                                               │
│ 6. SYNTHESIZE findings ("so what?")               │
│    ↓                                               │
│ 7. RECOMMEND actions                              │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

KEY MINDSET:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Hypothesis-driven (not boiling the ocean)
• Issue-focused (not data-driven)
• Top-down thinking (conclusion first)
• Fact-based (not opinion-based)
• Action-oriented (implementation focus)
```

### Step 1: Define the Problem

```
PROBLEM DEFINITION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The most critical step. A poorly defined problem
leads to wasted effort and wrong answers.

KEY QUESTION TECHNIQUE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Format: "What should [decision-maker] do about
        [situation] to achieve [objective]?"

Examples:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
✗ Weak: "How can we grow?"
✓ Strong: "What should the CEO do to grow revenue
          by 20% in 3 years while maintaining margins?"

✗ Weak: "What's wrong with our strategy?"
✓ Strong: "Should the company exit the consumer
          segment to focus on enterprise?"

PROBLEM DEFINITION CHECKLIST:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
□ Who is the decision-maker?
□ What is the specific decision or question?
□ What is the scope (products, geographies, time)?
□ What constraints exist?
□ What would success look like?
□ What is the timeline for the decision?
```

### Step 2: Structure the Analysis

```
THE MECE PRINCIPLE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

MUTUALLY EXCLUSIVE:
• No overlap between categories
• Each item belongs to one bucket only

COLLECTIVELY EXHAUSTIVE:
• All possibilities covered
• Nothing left out

EXAMPLE: Why are profits declining?
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MECE Structure:

PROFITS = REVENUE - COSTS

Revenue decline:
├── Price decline
│   ├── Competitive pressure
│   ├── Customer power
│   └── Product mix shift
├── Volume decline
│   ├── Market shrinking
│   ├── Market share loss
│   └── Customer churn
└── Mix shift to lower-margin products

Cost increase:
├── Variable costs up
│   ├── Input costs (materials, labor)
│   └── Operating inefficiency
└── Fixed costs up
    ├── Overhead growth
    └── Investment depreciation
```

### Common Issue Trees

```
PROFITABILITY ISSUE TREE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Profits
├── Revenues
│   ├── Price
│   │   ├── List price
│   │   └── Discounting
│   ├── Volume
│   │   ├── Market size
│   │   └── Market share
│   └── Mix
│       ├── Product mix
│       └── Channel mix
└── Costs
    ├── COGS
    │   ├── Materials
    │   ├── Labor
    │   └── Manufacturing overhead
    ├── SG&A
    │   ├── Sales
    │   ├── Marketing
    │   └── G&A
    └── Other
        ├── R&D
        └── Depreciation/Amortization

MARKET ENTRY ISSUE TREE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Should we enter?
├── Is the market attractive?
│   ├── Market size and growth
│   ├── Profitability
│   └── Competitive intensity
├── Can we win?
│   ├── Sustainable advantage possible?
│   ├── Required capabilities vs. ours
│   └── Competitive position achievable
└── Is the return sufficient?
    ├── Investment required
    ├── Expected returns
    └── Risk-adjusted analysis
```

## Hypothesis-Driven Thinking

### The Hypothesis Approach

```
TRADITIONAL vs. HYPOTHESIS-DRIVEN:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

TRADITIONAL:
Step 1: Gather all available data
Step 2: Analyze data
Step 3: Draw conclusions
Step 4: Make recommendations

Problem: Takes too long, boils the ocean

HYPOTHESIS-DRIVEN:
Step 1: Develop initial hypothesis (educated guess)
Step 2: Identify analyses to prove/disprove
Step 3: Gather ONLY relevant data
Step 4: Confirm or refine hypothesis
Step 5: Recommend

Advantage: Faster, more focused, action-oriented
```

### Developing Hypotheses

```
HYPOTHESIS FORMATION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Based on:
• Pattern recognition from experience
• Initial data review
• Expert interviews
• Industry analogies
• First principles logic

FORMAT:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"[Client] should do [X] because [reasons],
which will result in [outcomes]"

EXAMPLE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"TechCo should exit the hardware business and
focus on software because:
1. Hardware margins are declining
2. Software is growing faster
3. TechCo's software differentiation is stronger
4. Resources are spread too thin

This will increase ROIC from 8% to 15%"

TESTING:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For each reason, identify:
• What data would prove this true?
• What data would disprove it?
• How confident are we?
```

### Hypothesis Testing

```
PROVING OR DISPROVING:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Hypothesis: "We should acquire CompetitorX"

Sub-hypotheses to test:
1. "CompetitorX has valuable assets we need"
   Test: Asset analysis, capability assessment

2. "We can achieve synergies of $100M+"
   Test: Synergy bottoms-up analysis

3. "We can integrate successfully"
   Test: Cultural assessment, integration plan

4. "Price is reasonable"
   Test: Valuation, comparable transactions

ITERATIVE REFINEMENT:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• If evidence supports → Strengthen hypothesis
• If evidence contradicts → Revise hypothesis
• If mixed → Dig deeper or add nuance
```

## Prioritization and 80/20

### The 80/20 Principle

```
PARETO PRINCIPLE IN STRATEGY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
80% of value comes from 20% of analyses
80% of problems come from 20% of root causes
80% of results come from 20% of actions

IMPLICATION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Don't try to analyze everything equally
Focus on what matters most
Be willing to NOT answer some questions
Good enough beats perfect

PRIORITIZATION MATRIX:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                   EASE OF ANALYSIS
                   Easy           Hard
              ┌─────────────┬─────────────┐
         High │  DO FIRST   │  DO NEXT    │
  IMPACT      │  (Quick     │  (If time   │
              │   wins)     │   permits)  │
              ├─────────────┼─────────────┤
         Low  │  DELEGATE   │  DON'T DO   │
              │  OR QUICK   │  (Low ROI)  │
              └─────────────┴─────────────┘
```

### Focus on Key Drivers

```
KEY DRIVER IDENTIFICATION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Ask: Which few factors explain most of the outcome?

SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Variable        Range        Impact on Profit
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Price           ±5%          ±$15M (High)
Volume          ±10%         ±$8M (Medium)
Material cost   ±10%         ±$4M (Low)
Overhead        ±10%         ±$2M (Low)

FOCUS: Deep dive on price and volume
       Less time on materials and overhead

DRIVER TREES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Revenue = Price × Volume

Volume = Market Size × Market Share

Market Share = f(Product, Price, Promotion, Place)

Trace to actionable levers
Focus analysis on high-leverage factors
```

## Synthesis and "So What?"

### The Synthesis Pyramid

```
PYRAMID PRINCIPLE (Barbara Minto):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

               ┌─────────────────┐
               │ MAIN MESSAGE    │  Answer first
               │ (Recommendation)│
               └────────┬────────┘
                        │
        ┌───────────────┼───────────────┐
        │               │               │
   ┌────▼────┐    ┌─────▼─────┐    ┌────▼────┐
   │ Support │    │  Support  │    │ Support │
   │ Point 1 │    │  Point 2  │    │ Point 3 │
   └────┬────┘    └─────┬─────┘    └────┬────┘
        │               │               │
   ┌────▼────┐    ┌─────▼─────┐    ┌────▼────┐
   │ Data/   │    │  Data/    │    │ Data/   │
   │ Evidence│    │  Evidence │    │ Evidence│
   └─────────┘    └───────────┘    └─────────┘

RULE: Start with the answer, then support
      Don't build up to conclusion
```

### The "So What?" Test

```
SO WHAT? QUESTIONING:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Every finding must answer "So what?"

EXAMPLE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Finding: "Our market share declined 3%"
So what? "We're losing to competitor X in segment Y"
So what? "Our product is inferior on feature Z"
So what? "We should invest in R&D for feature Z"

Finding: "Customer satisfaction is 4.2/5"
So what? "That's above industry average of 3.8"
So what? "Satisfaction is not our problem"
So what? "Focus on acquisition, not retention"

ANALYSIS → FINDING → INSIGHT → IMPLICATION → ACTION

INSIGHT CREATION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Pattern recognition across data
• Connecting disparate findings
• Identifying root causes
• Seeing what others miss
• Quantifying the unquantified
```

### Creating Recommendations

```
ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CRITERIA FOR GOOD RECOMMENDATIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. SPECIFIC - Not vague or general
   ✗ "Improve customer service"
   ✓ "Reduce call wait time to <2 minutes"

2. MEASURABLE - Can track success
   ✗ "Become more innovative"
   ✓ "Launch 3 new products by Q4"

3. ACTIONABLE - Someone can do it
   ✗ "Have better culture"
   ✓ "Implement weekly team meetings"

4. PRIORITIZED - What first, second, third
   ✗ "Do all these things"
   ✓ "Phase 1: X, Phase 2: Y, Phase 3: Z"

5. OWNED - Clear accountability
   ✗ "The company should..."
   ✓ "The CMO should..."

RECOMMENDATION FORMAT:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What: Specific action
Why: Supporting rationale
How: Implementation approach
Who: Accountable person
When: Timeline
Impact: Expected benefit
Risk: Key concerns and mitigation
```

## Common Analytical Errors

### Pitfalls to Avoid

```
ANALYTICAL TRAPS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. ANALYSIS PARALYSIS
   Problem: Too much analysis, no decision
   Fix: Set deadlines, 80/20 focus

2. CONFIRMATION BIAS
   Problem: Seeking evidence for existing belief
   Fix: Actively seek disconfirming evidence

3. ANCHORING
   Problem: Over-weighting first data seen
   Fix: Consider range of scenarios

4. SUNK COST FALLACY
   Problem: "We've invested so much already..."
   Fix: Future-only analysis

5. GROUPTHINK
   Problem: Team agrees too quickly
   Fix: Assign devil's advocate, seek outsider view

6. BOILING THE OCEAN
   Problem: Trying to analyze everything
   Fix: Prioritize ruthlessly, accept uncertainty

7. CONCLUSION IN SEARCH OF DATA
   Problem: Fitting data to predetermined answer
   Fix: Let data lead, be willing to change
```

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Define the problem precisely** - Wrong question = wrong answer
2. **Structure MECE** - Mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive
3. **Develop hypotheses early** - Don't boil the ocean
4. **Prioritize ruthlessly** - 80/20 focus
5. **Test and iterate** - Refine hypotheses with evidence
6. **Synthesize to "so what?"** - Findings must have implications
7. **Recommend specifically** - Actionable, measurable, owned
8. **Avoid common traps** - Bias, paralysis, groupthink
