# Industry and Competitive Analysis Frameworks

## Learning Objectives

- Apply PESTEL analysis for macro-environmental scanning
- Conduct rigorous Five Forces industry analysis
- Map strategic groups and analyze competitive positions
- Perform systematic competitor analysis
- Assess industry lifecycle stage and implications

---

## Introduction

Understanding the external environment is the starting point for strategy. Before determining how to compete, strategists must understand where to compete—the industry structure, competitive dynamics, and environmental forces that shape opportunities and threats. This chapter covers the essential frameworks for external analysis.

---

## PESTEL Analysis

### Framework Overview

```
PESTEL FRAMEWORK:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Macro-environment analysis across six dimensions

P - POLITICAL
    • Government stability
    • Tax policy
    • Trade regulations
    • Labor laws
    • Political risk

E - ECONOMIC
    • GDP growth
    • Interest rates
    • Inflation
    • Exchange rates
    • Consumer confidence

S - SOCIAL
    • Demographics
    • Cultural trends
    • Lifestyle changes
    • Education levels
    • Health consciousness

T - TECHNOLOGICAL
    • R&D activity
    • Automation
    • Technology change rate
    • Digital transformation
    • Innovation trends

E - ENVIRONMENTAL
    • Climate change
    • Sustainability pressure
    • Resource scarcity
    • Environmental regulations
    • Carbon policies

L - LEGAL
    • Antitrust laws
    • Employment regulations
    • Consumer protection
    • Data privacy (GDPR)
    • Industry-specific regulations
```

### PESTEL Application

```
EXAMPLE: Electric Vehicle Industry PESTEL
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

POLITICAL:
• Government subsidies for EVs (+)
• Bans on ICE vehicles planned (+)
• Infrastructure investment (+)
• Trade tensions (chips) (-)

ECONOMIC:
• Rising fuel prices (+)
• High interest rates (-)
• Supply chain inflation (-)
• Energy cost volatility (+/-)

SOCIAL:
• Climate awareness (+)
• Range anxiety (-)
• Urban mobility trends (+)
• Status symbol appeal (+)

TECHNOLOGICAL:
• Battery technology advancing (+)
• Charging infrastructure improving (+)
• Autonomous driving integration (+)
• Software-defined vehicles (+)

ENVIRONMENTAL:
• Carbon reduction targets (+)
• Battery recycling concerns (-)
• Mining ethics concerns (-)
• Grid capacity questions (+/-)

LEGAL:
• Emission standards tightening (+)
• Safety regulations evolving (-)
• Charging standards (opportunity)
• Data privacy for connected cars (-)

OVERALL ASSESSMENT:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Strong tailwinds for EV industry
Key risks: Infrastructure, supply chain, regulation
```

## Porter's Five Forces (Detailed)

### Comprehensive Application

```
FIVE FORCES ANALYSIS TEMPLATE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

                     ┌─────────────────┐
                     │ THREAT OF NEW   │
                     │   ENTRANTS      │
                     └────────┬────────┘
                              │
                              ▼
    ┌──────────────┐    ┌──────────┐    ┌──────────────┐
    │   SUPPLIER   │    │          │    │    BUYER     │
    │    POWER     │───►│ INDUSTRY │◄───│    POWER     │
    │              │    │ RIVALRY  │    │              │
    └──────────────┘    └────┬─────┘    └──────────────┘
                             │
                             ▼
                     ┌───────────────┐
                     │  THREAT OF    │
                     │  SUBSTITUTES  │
                     └───────────────┘

FOR EACH FORCE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Identify specific factors
2. Assess strength (1-5)
3. Determine impact on profitability
4. Consider trends (increasing/decreasing)
```

### Force Analysis Details

```
THREAT OF NEW ENTRANTS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
KEY FACTORS:
• Economies of scale
• Capital requirements
• Switching costs
• Access to distribution
• Brand loyalty
• Government regulations
• Expected retaliation
• Proprietary technology

ASSESSMENT QUESTIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• How much capital required to enter?
• Can new entrants achieve scale quickly?
• Are customers locked in?
• Are channels accessible?
• Will incumbents fight back hard?

SUPPLIER POWER:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
KEY FACTORS:
• Supplier concentration
• Importance of volume to supplier
• Differentiation of inputs
• Switching costs
• Threat of forward integration
• Importance of industry to supplier

ASSESSMENT QUESTIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• How many suppliers exist?
• How differentiated are their products?
• What's the cost to switch suppliers?
• Could suppliers become competitors?

BUYER POWER:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
KEY FACTORS:
• Buyer concentration vs. industry
• Buyer volume
• Buyer switching costs
• Buyer information
• Ability to backward integrate
• Price sensitivity
• Product differentiation

ASSESSMENT QUESTIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• How concentrated are buyers?
• How important is our product to them?
• How easily can they switch?
• Do they have leverage in negotiations?

THREAT OF SUBSTITUTES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
KEY FACTORS:
• Relative price/performance
• Switching costs
• Buyer propensity to substitute

ASSESSMENT QUESTIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• What alternatives exist?
• How does value compare?
• Are substitutes improving?
• What would trigger switching?

INDUSTRY RIVALRY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
KEY FACTORS:
• Number of competitors
• Industry growth rate
• Fixed costs/value added
• Differentiation
• Switching costs
• Capacity increments
• Diversity of competitors
• Exit barriers

ASSESSMENT QUESTIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• How many competitors, what size?
• Is the market growing or declining?
• How differentiated are products?
• How easy to exit?
```

### Five Forces Scoring

```
QUANTITATIVE ASSESSMENT:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Force               Score (1-5)    Impact
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Entry threat            3          Moderate
Supplier power          2          Low
Buyer power            4          High
Substitutes            3          Moderate
Rivalry                5          Very High
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Average threat level:   3.4

INTERPRETATION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1.0-2.0: Highly attractive (low competitive intensity)
2.0-3.0: Attractive (moderate intensity)
3.0-4.0: Average attractiveness
4.0-5.0: Unattractive (high intensity)

PROFITABILITY IMPLICATION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Higher threat level → Lower industry profitability
Lower threat level → Higher industry profitability
```

## Strategic Group Mapping

### Framework Overview

```
STRATEGIC GROUP DEFINITION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Clusters of firms following similar strategies
Compete more intensely within groups
Mobility barriers between groups

MAPPING DIMENSIONS (choose two):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Price/quality level
• Geographic scope (local/regional/national/global)
• Vertical integration degree
• Product line breadth
• Distribution channels
• Technology/innovation level
• Brand/marketing intensity
• Cost position
```

GROUPS IDENTIFIED:

## Competitor Analysis

### Systematic Competitor Assessment

```
COMPETITOR ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. IDENTIFICATION
   • Direct competitors
   • Indirect competitors
   • Potential entrants
   • Substitute providers

2. ASSESSMENT DIMENSIONS
   ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │ Current Strategy                                │
   │ • What are they doing?                         │
   │ • How are they competing?                      │
   │ • What's working/not working?                  │
   ├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
   │ Objectives                                      │
   │ • What are their goals?                        │
   │ • Growth, profitability, market share?         │
   │ • What drives their decisions?                 │
   ├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
   │ Assumptions                                     │
   │ • What do they believe about industry?         │
   │ • About themselves?                            │
   │ • About us?                                    │
   ├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
   │ Capabilities                                    │
   │ • Core competencies?                           │
   │ • Strengths and weaknesses?                    │
   │ • Resources available?                         │
   └─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

3. RESPONSE PROFILE
   • What will competitor do in future?
   • How will they respond to our moves?
   • What triggers action?
```

## Industry Lifecycle Analysis

### Lifecycle Stages

```
INDUSTRY LIFECYCLE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Revenue/
Profit   │
         │                     ┌────────┐
         │                    /│ MATURE │\
         │                   / └────────┘ \
         │        ┌────────┐/              \┌────────┐
         │       /│ GROWTH │                │DECLINE │
         │      / └────────┘                └────────┘
         │     /
         │    / ┌────────┐
         │   /  │EMERGING│
         │  /   └────────┘
         └────────────────────────────────────────────▶
                                            Time

STAGE CHARACTERISTICS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
             Emerging    Growth      Mature      Decline
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Growth      Very high   High        Moderate    Negative
Competition Low         Rising      Intense     Falling
Margins     Variable    High        Declining   Low
Focus       Product     Capacity    Efficiency  Cash
Strategy    Innovate    Scale       Defend      Exit?
```

### Strategic Implications by Stage

```
EMERGING STAGE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• High uncertainty
• First-mover advantages possible
• Focus on product development
• Build market awareness
• Invest heavily

GROWTH STAGE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Rapid expansion
• Build capacity ahead of demand
• Develop channels
• Establish brand
• Acquire share

MATURE STAGE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Focus on efficiency
• Defend market position
• Customer retention critical
• Innovation in processes
• Consider consolidation

DECLINE STAGE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Harvest or divest
• Focus on profitable segments
• Reduce investment
• Consider exit
• Manage decline efficiently
```

## Key Takeaways

1. **PESTEL scans macro-environment** - Political, economic, social, technological, environmental, legal
2. **Five Forces determine industry profitability** - Structure shapes performance
3. **Strategic groups reveal competitive clusters** - Competition varies within industry
4. **Competitor analysis predicts behavior** - Strategy, objectives, assumptions, capabilities
5. **Lifecycle stage affects strategy** - Different stages need different approaches
6. **Combine frameworks for full picture** - No single framework is sufficient
7. **Dynamic analysis matters** - How forces are changing, not just current state
8. **Quantify where possible** - Numbers add rigor to qualitative assessment
