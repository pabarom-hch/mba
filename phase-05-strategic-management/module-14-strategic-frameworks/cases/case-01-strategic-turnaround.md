# Case Study: Strategic Turnaround - Comprehensive Analysis Exercise

## Executive Summary

This case provides a complete strategic analysis exercise requiring the integration of multiple frameworks. Students will analyze a struggling retail company facing disruption, diagnose the strategic issues, and develop a turnaround strategy using the full toolkit of strategic frameworks.

**Key Question:** Using comprehensive strategic analysis, what should the CEO of a struggling department store chain do to return the company to profitable growth?

## Analysis Assignment

### Part A: External Analysis (25 points)

1. Complete a Five Forces analysis. Rate each force (1-5) and provide specific evidence.

2. Identify the 3 most critical PESTEL factors affecting TradiMart and explain their strategic implications.

3. Analyze TradiMart's strategic group position. What mobility barriers exist between groups?

4. Assess 2 key competitors using the competitor analysis framework. Predict their likely strategic moves.

### Part B: Internal Analysis (25 points)

1. Apply the VRIO framework to TradiMart's resources and capabilities. Does TradiMart have any competitive advantages?

2. Complete a 7S analysis. Identify the 3 most critical misalignments.

3. Assess TradiMart's value chain. Where are the key weaknesses and what is their impact?

4. What core competencies, if any, does TradiMart possess? What competencies does it need?

### Part C: Strategic Synthesis (25 points)

1. Develop a comprehensive SWOT analysis based on your external and internal analyses.

2. Use the TOWS matrix to generate at least 4 strategic options.

3. Evaluate each option against strategic criteria (feasibility, attractiveness, fit). Recommend one strategy.

4. If this were a BCG or GE-McKinsey analysis, how would you characterize TradiMart? What does this imply?

### Part D: Implementation (25 points)

1. Develop a 100-day plan for your recommended strategy with specific milestones.

2. Apply Kotter's 8-step model. What are the critical change management challenges?

3. Design a balanced scorecard for tracking turnaround progress.

4. What are the top 3 risks to successful implementation and how would you mitigate them?

## Teaching Note

### Learning Objectives

This case teaches students to:
- Integrate multiple strategic frameworks
- Conduct comprehensive strategic analysis
- Synthesize external and internal analyses
- Develop turnaround strategies
- Create implementable recommendations

### Key Insights

**Diagnosis:**
- TradiMart is stuck in the middle (mid-tier in declining segment)
- No competitive advantage (fails VRIO on all dimensions)
- Severe organizational misalignment (7S)
- Financially distressed (limited runway)
- External environment highly challenging

**Viable Strategic Options:**

1. **Controlled Retreat + Digital Focus**
   - Close 150+ weakest stores
   - Invest savings in e-commerce
   - Focus on best locations
   - Reduce to core categories

2. **Category Specialization**
   - Exit declining categories (home, kids)
   - Double down on cosmetics and women's apparel
   - Partner with strong brands
   - Create store-in-store concepts

3. **Value Repositioning**
   - Move down-market to compete with off-price
   - Reduce prices and service
   - Faster inventory turns
   - Different customer

4. **Strategic Sale/Merger**
   - Merge with Macy's or Kohl's
   - Provide scale synergies
   - Monetize real estate
   - Preserve some jobs

**Recommended Strategy:**
Option 1 (Controlled Retreat + Digital) is most viable because:
- Stops bleeding (close unprofitable stores)
- Invests in future (digital)
- Realistic given financial constraints
- Preserves core business
- Creates option value

### Implementation Challenges

- Change management: Legacy culture resists
- Capital constraints: Limited investment capacity
- Talent: Need digital skills company doesn't have
- Time pressure: Cannot survive extended transition
- Stakeholder management: Employees, landlords, suppliers
