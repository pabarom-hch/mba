## Introduction

Working capital optimization converts operational improvements directly into cash. Every day of DSO reduced or DPO extended releases cash that can fund growth, reduce debt, or return to investors. For a $100M revenue company, improving the cash conversion cycle by 10 days can release $2.7M in cash. This lesson covers the systematic approach to working capital improvement.

## Core Concepts

### Cash Conversion Cycle

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                   CASH CONVERSION CYCLE                      │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                              │
│     ┌─────────┐      ┌─────────┐      ┌─────────┐          │
│     │ BUY     │      │ SELL    │      │ COLLECT │          │
│     │Materials│ ───► │ Product │ ───► │ Cash    │          │
│     └─────────┘      └─────────┘      └─────────┘          │
│          │                │                │                │
│          ▼                ▼                ▼                │
│                                                              │
│   DIO              DSO              DPO                     │
│   (Days Inventory  (Days Sales     (Days Payables          │
│    Outstanding)    Outstanding)    Outstanding)            │
│                                                              │
│   ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐ │
│   │                                                       │ │
│   │   CCC = DIO + DSO - DPO                              │ │
│   │                                                       │ │
│   │   Example:                                            │ │
│   │   DIO = 45 days                                       │ │
│   │   DSO = 55 days                                       │ │
│   │   DPO = 35 days                                       │ │
│   │   CCC = 45 + 55 - 35 = 65 days                       │ │
│   │                                                       │ │
│   └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘ │
│                                                              │
│   CASH IMPACT                                               │
│   ───────────                                               │
│   Each 1 day improvement = Revenue ÷ 365 = Cash released   │
│   $100M revenue, 1 day = $274K cash                         │
│                                                              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Working Capital Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Target Direction |
|--------|---------|------------------|
| **DSO** | (AR / Revenue) × 365 | Decrease |
| **DIO** | (Inventory / COGS) × 365 | Decrease |
| **DPO** | (AP / COGS) × 365 | Increase |
| **CCC** | DIO + DSO - DPO | Decrease |
| **NWC % Revenue** | NWC / Revenue | Decrease |

## Framework: Accounts Receivable Optimization

### AR Diagnostic

```
AR DIAGNOSTIC FRAMEWORK
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PERFORMANCE METRICS
├── DSO trend (12-24 months)
├── DSO vs. terms (gap = problem)
├── Aging buckets (current, 30, 60, 90+)
├── Bad debt write-offs
└── Collection effectiveness index

PROCESS ASSESSMENT
├── Credit approval process
├── Invoice accuracy and timing
├── Collection process
├── Dispute resolution
└── Cash application

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
├── Customer concentration
├── Industry payment norms
├── Invoice quality issues
├── Credit policy gaps
└── Collection resource constraints
```

### AR Improvement Levers

| Lever | Description | Impact |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| **Invoice timing** | Bill immediately, accurately | 2-5 days DSO |
| **Electronic invoicing** | Faster delivery, fewer disputes | 2-3 days DSO |
| **Payment terms** | Tighten terms, enforce | 5-15 days DSO |
| **Collection process** | Systematic follow-up | 5-10 days DSO |
| **Early pay discounts** | 2/10 net 30 | 3-5 days DSO |
| **Credit policy** | Tighter credit limits | Risk reduction |

### Collection Process Best Practices

| Timing | Action |
|--------|--------|
| **Invoice date** | Send accurate invoice immediately |
| **7 days** | Confirm receipt |
| **Terms due** | Reminder before due |
| **1 day late** | Call, email follow-up |
| **15 days late** | Escalation to sales |
| **30 days late** | Payment plan or credit hold |
| **60 days late** | External collection consideration |

## Framework: Accounts Payable Optimization

### AP Diagnostic

| Assessment Area | Key Questions |
|-----------------|---------------|
| **Payment terms** | What are standard terms? Negotiated? |
| **Payment timing** | Do we pay early? On time? Late? |
| **Process efficiency** | Invoice approval cycle? |
| **Discounts** | Are we capturing early pay discounts? |
| **Vendor concentration** | Who are the big vendors? |

### AP Improvement Levers

```
AP OPTIMIZATION STRATEGIES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. TERM EXTENSION
   ├── Negotiate longer standard terms
   ├── Net 30 → Net 45/60
   └── Impact: 15-30 days DPO

2. PAYMENT TIMING
   ├── Pay on due date, not before
   ├── Eliminate early payment
   └── Impact: 5-15 days DPO

3. DISCOUNT ANALYSIS
   ├── Evaluate 2/10 net 30 economics
   ├── Take if cost of capital > 36% annualized
   └── Otherwise, pay at net

4. SUPPLY CHAIN FINANCE
   ├── Reverse factoring programs
   ├── Vendor financing
   └── Extends payment without hurting vendors

5. PROCESS EFFICIENCY
   ├── Reduce approval bottlenecks
   ├── Electronic invoicing
   └── Enables strategic payment timing
```

### Discount Economics

**2/10 Net 30 Example:**
- 2% discount for paying in 10 days vs. 30 days
- You're "paying" 2% to use money for 20 extra days
- Annualized: (2% / 98%) × (365 / 20) = **37.2%**

| If Cost of Capital Is... | Take Discount? |
|--------------------------|----------------|
| < 37% | Yes, take discount |
| > 37% | No, pay at net 30 |

## Worked Example: Distribution Company Working Capital

**Situation:** $100M revenue distributor, struggling with cash

**Working Capital Diagnostic:**

| Metric | Current | Benchmark | Gap |
|--------|---------|-----------|-----|
| DSO | 62 days | 45 days | 17 days |
| DIO | 55 days | 40 days | 15 days |
| DPO | 28 days | 40 days | (12 days) |
| CCC | 89 days | 45 days | 44 days |

**Cash Impact of Gap:**
- $100M revenue ÷ 365 = $274K per day
- 44 days × $274K = **$12.1M excess working capital**

**Improvement Program:**

| Initiative | DSO | DIO | DPO | Days Improved | Cash Released |
|------------|-----|-----|-----|---------------|---------------|
| Collection process | -10 | — | — | 10 | $2.74M |
| Payment terms enforcement | -5 | — | — | 5 | $1.37M |
| Demand forecasting | — | -8 | — | 8 | $2.19M |
| SKU rationalization | — | -5 | — | 5 | $1.37M |
| Vendor term extension | — | — | +8 | 8 | $2.19M |
| Payment timing | — | — | +4 | 4 | $1.10M |
| **Total** | **-15** | **-13** | **+12** | **40** | **$10.96M** |

## PE Application

### Working Capital Best Practices

| Practice | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| **Start with quick wins** | Collection focus, payment timing |
| **Set specific targets** | Days targets by metric |
| **Weekly monitoring** | Cash flow visibility |
| **Tie to incentives** | Include in management comp |
| **Invest in systems** | ERP enables optimization |
| **Maintain relationships** | Balance optimization with partners |

### Common Working Capital Mistakes

| Mistake | Impact | Prevention |
|---------|--------|------------|
| Aggressive collection | Customer relationship damage | Segment approach |
| Extreme AP stretch | Supplier relationship damage | Negotiate first |
| Inventory whiplash | Stock-outs, lost sales | Gradual optimization |
| Ignoring seasonality | Working capital volatility | Plan for peaks |
| One-time focus | Gains slip away | Process and system changes |

## CEO Application

### Building Working Capital Discipline

**For CEOs driving working capital improvement:**

1. **Make it visible** — Weekly cash flow review
2. **Own the big accounts** — CEO involvement on major AR/AP
3. **Set clear policies** — Credit terms, payment terms
4. **Invest in process** — Systems enable sustainability
5. **Balance optimization** — Maintain key relationships

## Key Takeaways

1. **Working capital = cash** — Every day matters
2. **AR is often the biggest opportunity** — Collection process improvement
3. **AP requires balance** — Optimize without damaging relationships
4. **Inventory is complex** — Data and systems required
5. **Process enables sustainability** — One-time efforts fade
6. **Weekly visibility** — Cash needs constant attention
