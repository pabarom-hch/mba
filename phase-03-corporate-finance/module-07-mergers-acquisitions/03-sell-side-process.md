# Sell-Side Process

## Learning Objectives

- Understand when and why to sell a company
- Design and execute effective sale processes
- Maximize value through competitive dynamics
- Manage stakeholders during a sale
- Navigate confidentiality and timing considerations

---

## Introduction

Selling a company or division is often a once-in-career event for executives and a transformational moment for shareholders. The sell-side process requires balancing value maximization with execution certainty, all while managing confidentiality and stakeholder expectations. Proper process design and execution can add 10-20% to transaction value.

---

## Core Concepts

### Reasons to Sell

```
Strategic Reasons:
├── Non-core business/Division
├── Capital reallocation
├── Regulatory requirement
├── Focus on core operations
└── Strategic pivot

Financial Reasons:
├── Maximize shareholder value
├── Liquidity for shareholders
├── Deleveraging/Debt reduction
├── Valuation premium available
└── Capital for growth investments

Circumstantial Reasons:
├── Unsolicited offer received
├── Succession/Estate planning
├── Founder liquidity
├── Partnership disputes
└── Distress situations
```

### Process Options

| Process Type | Description | Best For |
|--------------|-------------|----------|
| Broad Auction | 15-30 buyers contacted | Maximize price |
| Limited Auction | 5-10 selected buyers | Balance price/confidentiality |
| Targeted Process | 2-3 most likely buyers | Confidentiality critical |
| Negotiated Sale | Single buyer | Strong relationship, speed |
| Dual-Track | Sale + IPO preparation | Maximize optionality |

### Process Timeline

```
Preparation Phase (Weeks 1-8)
├── Engage advisors
├── Prepare materials
├── Develop buyer list
└── Management preparation

Marketing Phase (Weeks 9-16)
├── Contact buyers
├── Distribute teaser/CIM
├── Manage Q&A
└── Receive initial indications

Due Diligence Phase (Weeks 17-24)
├── Select proceeding buyers
├── Data room access
├── Management presentations
└── Site visits

Final Phase (Weeks 25-32)
├── Final bids
├── Select winner
├── Negotiate definitive agreement
└── Sign

Closing Phase (Weeks 33-40+)
├── Regulatory approvals
├── Financing conditions
├── Closing mechanics
└── Transition
```

## Preparation Phase

### Selecting Advisors

**Investment Bank Selection:**

```
Selection Criteria:
├── Sector expertise
├── Relevant transaction experience
├── Relationships with likely buyers
├── Team quality and bandwidth
├── Fee structure
└── Reputation

Fee Structures:
├── Success fee: 1-3% of transaction value
├── Monthly retainer: $50K-$150K (credited against success fee)
├── Minimum fee: Often specified
├── Tail provision: Fee protection post-engagement
```

**Other Advisors:**

```
Legal Counsel:
├── Transaction experience
├── Knowledge of regulatory issues
├── Documentation efficiency

Accounting:
├── Quality of earnings support
├── Tax structuring
├── Financial statements preparation

Other Specialists:
├── Environmental consultants
├── Intellectual property counsel
├── Employee benefits advisors
```

### Preparing Marketing Materials

**Teaser (1-2 pages):**

```
Contents:
- Anonymous company description
- Industry and business overview
- Key financial highlights
- Transaction summary
- Contact information

Purpose:
- Generate initial interest
- Protect confidentiality
- Qualify buyer interest
```

**Confidential Information Memorandum (CIM):**

```
Typical Structure (50-100 pages):

1. Executive Summary (5 pages)
   - Investment highlights
   - Key financials
   - Transaction overview

2. Industry Overview (10 pages)
   - Market size and growth
   - Competitive landscape
   - Industry trends

3. Company Overview (15 pages)
   - History and evolution
   - Business model
   - Products/services
   - Operations

4. Financial Performance (15 pages)
   - Historical results
   - Financial projections
   - Key assumptions

5. Growth Opportunities (10 pages)
   - Strategic initiatives
   - Expansion plans
   - Upside potential

6. Management Team (5 pages)
   - Key personnel
   - Organizational structure

7. Appendix
   - Detailed financials
   - Customer information
   - Facility details
```

### Developing the Buyer List

```
Strategic Buyers:

Financial Buyers:

Criteria for Prioritization:
```


### Management Presentations

```
Typical Format (4-6 hours):

1. Business Overview (1 hour)
   - CEO presents strategy and vision

2. Financial Deep Dive (1.5 hours)
   - CFO presents historical and projections

3. Operations (1 hour)
   - COO/functional leads

4. Q&A (1-2 hours)
   - Open discussion

Preparation:
- Anticipate questions
- Align on talking points
- Practice delivery
- Prepare backup materials
```

## Negotiation and Closing

### Evaluating Bids

**Bid Comparison Matrix:**

| Factor | Bidder A | Bidder B | Bidder C |
|--------|----------|----------|----------|
| Price | $500M | $480M | $520M |
| Structure | All cash | Cash + stock | All cash |
| Financing | Committed | Subject to | Committed |
| Conditions | Few | Many | Moderate |
| Regulatory | Clear | Complex | Clear |
| Certainty | High | Medium | High |
| Timeline | 60 days | 90 days | 75 days |

**Weighting Factors:**

```
Typical Weighting:

Price: 50%
├── Base price
├── Contingent payments
└── Structure value

Certainty: 30%
├── Financing
├── Regulatory
├── Conditions

Terms: 20%
├── Indemnification
├── Employee treatment
├── Transition services
```

### Negotiating the Purchase Agreement

**Key Terms:**

```
Price and Payment:
├── Purchase price
├── Working capital adjustment
├── Earnouts (if any)
├── Escrow arrangements
└── Payment mechanics

Representations and Warranties:
├── Scope and coverage
├── Disclosure schedules
├── Knowledge qualifiers
├── Materiality thresholds
└── Survival periods

Indemnification:
├── Caps and baskets
├── Survival periods
├── Special indemnities
├── Escrow/holdback

Covenants:
├── Pre-closing conduct
├── No-shop provisions
├── Regulatory cooperation
├── Employee matters
```

### Creating Competition

**Techniques:**

```
1. Structured Process
   - Clear deadlines
   - Comparable information
   - Level playing field

2. Strategic Information
   - Share buyer interest (appropriately)
   - Indicate competitive tension
   - Create urgency

3. Alternative Options
   - Dual-track with IPO
   - Refinancing alternative
   - Willingness to walk

4. Timing Leverage
   - Strategic timing
   - External deadlines
   - Market windows
```

## Special Situations

### Management Buyout (MBO)

```
Considerations:
├── Management participation
├── Conflict management
├── Board independence
├── Process fairness
└── Fiduciary duties

Best Practices:
- Independent board committee
- Separate advisors
- Market check/go-shop
- Majority of minority approval
```

### Carve-Out/Divestiture

```
Additional Complexity:
├── Standalone financials (carve-out)
├── Transition services agreement
├── Stranded costs
├── IP/Asset allocation
├── Employee selection

Key Documents:
- Carve-out P&L
- Pro forma balance sheet
- TSA framework
- Intellectual property schedule
```

### Distressed Sale

```
Different Dynamics:
├── Speed critical
├── Limited process
├── Lower prices
├── Liability concerns
├── Creditor involvement

Options:
- Asset sale (Section 363)
- Going concern sale
- Orderly liquidation
- Structured transaction
```

## Worked Example

### Sell-Side Process: MediaCo Divestiture

**Situation:**
Conglomerate selling MediaCo division ($300M revenue, $45M EBITDA)

**Week 1-6: Preparation**
```
Advisor selection: Global investment bank
Materials prepared:
- 80-page CIM
- Financial model
- Management presentation

Buyer list developed:
- 8 strategic buyers
- 12 financial sponsors
- 5 international
```

**Week 7-12: Marketing**
```
Teaser distributed: 25 recipients
NDAs signed: 18
CIMs distributed: 18
Initial indications received: 8

Range: $350M - $450M
Proceeding to Phase 2: 5 bidders
```

**Week 13-18: Due Diligence**
```
Data room opened
Management presentations: 5 meetings
Site visits completed: 4

Issues identified:
- Customer concentration (25% top customer)
- Pension underfunding ($5M)
- Environmental matter (contained)
```

**Week 19-22: Final Bids**
```
Final bids received: 4

| Bidder | Price | Structure | Certainty |
|--------|-------|-----------|-----------|
| Strategic A | $420M | All cash | High |
| Strategic B | $400M | Cash | High |
| PE Firm | $410M | Cash | Medium |
| Int'l Strategic | $435M | Cash + stock | Medium |

Analysis: Strategic A preferred
- $420M competitive price
- All cash certainty
- Committed financing
- Clean regulatory path
```

**Week 23-28: Documentation**
```
Negotiations:
- Working capital: $25M target
- Indemnity cap: 10% ($42M)
- Escrow: 5% for 18 months
- Rep insurance purchased

Agreement signed: Week 28
```

**Week 29-36: Closing**
```
- HSR filed and cleared (30 days)
- Third-party consents obtained
- Financing closed
- Employee communications
- Close: Week 36
```

## Key Takeaways

1. **Preparation drives value** - Well-prepared sale commands premium
2. **Process creates competition** - Multiple bidders maximize price
3. **Materials matter** - Quality CIM differentiates
4. **Manage information carefully** - Confidentiality protects
5. **Certainty has value** - Not just about price
6. **Stakeholder management critical** - Employees, customers, board
7. **Expect surprises** - Flexibility in process design
