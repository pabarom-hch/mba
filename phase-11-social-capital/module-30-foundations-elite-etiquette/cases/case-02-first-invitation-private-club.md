# Case Study: First Invitation to a Private Club

## Case Overview

- **Setting:** The Metropolitan Club, New York City
- **Key Players:** Sarah Martinez, Partner at a consulting firm; James Ashford III, long-time member and potential client
- **The Challenge:** Navigating an unfamiliar environment while building a professional relationship
- **Stakes:** A potential eight-figure consulting engagement and entry into a new social circle

---

## Background

**Sarah Martinez** is a 42-year-old partner at McKinsey & Company. She grew up in Houston, daughter of first-generation immigrants, and rose through the firm through talent and hard work. She's now a senior partner in the private equity practice and one of the firm's most successful rainmakers.

Sarah has been introduced to **James Ashford III**, the CEO of Ashford Holdings, a family-owned conglomerate with interests in real estate, manufacturing, and media. The Ashford family has been prominent in New York since the early 1900s. James is the third generation to lead the company.

After an initial office meeting that went well, James has invited Sarah to lunch at the Metropolitan Club to continue their conversation.

**The Metropolitan Club:**
- Founded in 1891 by J.P. Morgan
- One of Manhattan's most prestigious private clubs
- Known for strict traditions and formal atmosphere
- Dress code strictly enforced
- Cell phones prohibited in most areas
- Members-only; guests must be accompanied

---

## The Situation

### The Invitation

James's assistant calls Sarah's assistant to arrange lunch. The details:
- Date: Tuesday, one week hence
- Time: 12:30 PM
- Location: The Metropolitan Club, 1 East 60th Street
- Note: "Mr. Ashford suggests meeting in the main lobby"

Sarah has never been to the Metropolitan Club. She knows it only by reputation.

### The Day Of

**Arrival:**
Sarah arrives at 12:25 PM. The exterior is a Stanford White palazzo facing Central Park. She enters through the main door into a grand marble lobby. A liveried doorman greets her; she gives James's name.

"Mrs. Martinez, Mr. Ashford is expecting you. Please wait here, and he'll be down shortly."

Sarah waits in the lobby, taking in the oil paintings, marble columns, and guests in suits and conservative dresses moving through the space. Five minutes later, James appears.

"Sarah, wonderful to see you. Thank you for coming. Let me show you upstairs."

**The Club:**

James leads Sarah up a sweeping staircase to the main dining room—a vast space with 30-foot ceilings, crystal chandeliers, and tables spaced far apart for privacy. A maître d' greets James by name and leads them to a corner table.

Sarah notices:
- She's one of few women in the room, though not the only one
- Everyone is dressed formally—men in suits, women in business attire
- Conversation is muted
- No visible phones anywhere
- Wait staff are formal and attentive

**The Meal:**

James orders a gin and tonic; Sarah orders sparkling water.

The menu has no prices. James recommends the sole.

For the first 30 minutes, they discuss:
- James's recent trip to London
- Sarah's daughter's college search
- A mutual acquaintance from the business world
- The club's history (James's grandfather was a founding member)

Eventually, James raises the business topic:
"I've been thinking about what you said regarding our portfolio optimization. Tell me more about how McKinsey might approach this."

## Discussion Questions

### Question 1: Preparation

How should Sarah have prepared for this lunch? What research is appropriate, and what would be over-preparation?

**Consider:**
- Dress code research
- Club etiquette (what to know, what to expect)
- Research on James vs. research on the club
- Mental preparation for the environment

### Question 2: Early Impressions

What should Sarah do in the first few minutes—from lobby to table? What mistakes might she make?

**Consider:**
- Waiting in the lobby (what to do, what not to do)
- Walking through the club with James
- Being seated, ordering
- Phone habits, physical presence

### Question 3: The Conversation Balance

How should Sarah handle the 30 minutes of personal conversation before business? Is this "wasted time"?

**Consider:**
- The purpose of relationship-building
- What James is evaluating
- How Sarah can contribute without dominating
- Topics to embrace or avoid

### Question 4: Business in a Social Setting

When James raises business, how should Sarah's approach differ from an office meeting?

**Consider:**
- Tone and pace appropriate to the setting
- Materials (should she have brought anything?)
- Hard sell vs. soft discussion
- Next steps and follow-up

## Analysis

### What Sarah Did Well

**In this scenario, let's assume Sarah:**

**Prepared appropriately:**
- Researched the club's dress code (chose a classic suit, pearls, conservative)
- Did not over-research the club's history (let James share that naturally)
- Refreshed her memory on James and the Ashford company
- Arrived five minutes early

**Navigated arrival gracefully:**
- Stood confidently (not nervously) while waiting
- Did not photograph the lobby or check her phone
- Greeted James warmly but not effusively
- Let him lead, followed his pace

**Handled the social conversation well:**
- Engaged genuinely on non-business topics
- Asked questions about James's London trip
- Shared appropriately about her daughter
- Listened more than she spoke
- Did not try to rush to business

**Transitioned to business appropriately:**
- Waited for James to raise the topic
- Discussed consultatively, not as a pitch
- Kept it conversational rather than presenting
- Did not promise or oversell
- Let him decide when to move on

### What Could Go Wrong

**Common mistakes in this scenario:**

**Phone visibility:**
Even having her phone on the table (off or silent) would violate club norms and signal unawareness.

**Photographing:**
Taking any photos—of the lobby, dining room, or meal—would be gauche.

**Being impressed:**
"This place is incredible!" would mark her as unfamiliar with such environments.

**Over-researching:**
Mentioning details about the club's history before James did would seem like stalking.

**Ordering awkwardly:**
Asking about prices, ordering the most expensive item, or asking "What's good?" would all signal unfamiliarity.

**Rushing to business:**
Trying to move past the personal conversation would signal transactional thinking.

**Pitching hard:**
Using the business discussion to deliver a rehearsed pitch would be wrong for the setting.

**Asking for the engagement:**
Pushing for next steps or asking "So, should we work together?" would be premature.

### The Nuances of Club Dining

**The No-Price Menu:**
Classic private clubs often don't include prices. The expectation: money isn't a consideration for decision-making. Don't ask about prices; order what you want or take the host's recommendation.

**The Host's Role:**
James will sign the check (no bill will come to the table). Sarah should not offer to pay or "get the next one"—this is his club, and he's hosting.

**The Thank You:**
After the lunch, Sarah should send a handwritten thank-you note within 48 hours. Not email. Handwritten, on quality stationery.

## Key Lessons

### Lesson 1: You're Being Evaluated Constantly

The lunch isn't just a meeting—it's an audition. James is assessing:
- Can Sarah navigate his world?
- Would she represent well to his board, his family?
- Is she someone he wants a long-term relationship with?
- Does she have the sophistication for sensitive work?

**Implication:** Everything matters, not just the business discussion.

### Lesson 2: Social Competence Is Professional Competence

In high-level consulting, you're often in social situations with clients—dinners, events, boards. Social grace is a professional qualification.

**Implication:** Invest in learning these skills as seriously as technical skills.

### Lesson 3: Less Is More

Sarah's best strategy:
- Conservative dress (don't try to impress)
- Genuine engagement (don't try to perform)
- Appropriate restraint (don't oversell)
- Graceful following (let James lead)

**Implication:** The safest path is the quietest path.

### Lesson 4: The Relationship Precedes the Transaction

James could hire McKinsey based on reputation alone. The lunch isn't about whether McKinsey is good—it's about whether Sarah is someone James wants to work with personally.

**Implication:** Focus on the relationship, not the sale.

## Epilogue

Three weeks after the lunch, James called Sarah directly. "I've been thinking about our conversation. I'd like to have you present to our board about the portfolio review. Can you come to our board meeting next month?"

Sarah presented to the Ashford Holdings board—a group that included three family members, two former CEOs, and a former Cabinet secretary. The engagement was approved.

Over the following two years, Sarah was invited to several more club lunches, a dinner at the Ashford home, and eventually, the Ashford Family Foundation's annual gala. The consulting engagement became one of McKinsey's largest in the private equity space.

James later told a mutual friend: "What I appreciated about Sarah was that she didn't try too hard. She fit right in without pretending to be someone she wasn't."

## Practical Takeaways

### Before a Club Lunch

- [ ] Research dress code (or ask directly: "Is there a dress code I should know?")
- [ ] Turn off phone before entering (or leave in car)
- [ ] Prepare for extended personal conversation
- [ ] Review host's recent activities/interests
- [ ] Have quality stationery ready for follow-up note

### During the Lunch

- [ ] Follow the host's lead on everything
- [ ] Do not photograph anything
- [ ] Order what host recommends or mid-range
- [ ] Engage genuinely in non-business talk
- [ ] Let host raise business when ready
- [ ] Keep business discussion conversational
- [ ] Do not push for next steps

### After the Lunch

- [ ] Send handwritten thank-you note within 48 hours
- [ ] Mention something specific from the conversation
- [ ] Do not include any business requests in the note
- [ ] Wait for them to initiate next steps
- [ ] Follow up in 2-3 weeks only if you haven't heard
