## Answer Key

### Part 1: Scenario Analysis

**Scenario 1: C**
"How do you do, Mrs. Whitmore" is the appropriately formal greeting for meeting an older person of stature for the first time. Options A and B are too casual/effusive ("great," "amazing," "incredible"). Option D immediately makes it about you.

**Scenario 2: B**
Declining to discuss someone's personal matters when they're not present demonstrates discretion. Deflecting to a neutral topic allows the conversation to continue gracefully. Options A and C engage in gossip; Option D solicits it.

**Scenario 3: C**
Simple, genuine appreciation without excess. Option A violates privacy expectations. Option B is over-effusive. Option D asks an inappropriate question about the property.

**Scenario 4: D**
Deflecting from the price question to personal significance. Option A violates the "never discuss prices" principle. Option B is a lie (problematic) but better than A/C. Option C still acknowledges price/value.

**Scenario 5: B**
Let the host set the pace. The 25 minutes of personal conversation is relationship-building, not wasted time. Options A and C rush inappropriately. Option D is manipulative.

### Part 2: Cultural Comprehension

**Question 1:** Stealth wealth means high quality without visible branding or obvious display. It's valued because it signals security—those who are truly wealthy don't need external validation or recognition, so choosing invisibility demonstrates that wealth is normal to you, not something requiring proof.

**Question 2:** The primary psychological difference is security. Old money feels secure because wealth has been present for generations; there's nothing to prove. New money often carries residual insecurity from the achievement phase—the need for validation, fear of loss, and desire to demonstrate success. This manifests as display (new money) vs. understatement (old money).

**Question 3:** Discretion is currency because it's the prerequisite for trust, and trust is how real relationships and opportunities emerge in elite circles. Demonstrating discretion signals that you can be trusted with sensitive information—about deals, families, personal matters—which is essential for being welcomed into inner circles.

**Question 4:** Fussell means that worrying about whether you belong proves you don't—yet. The secure don't think about their status because it's not in question. Visibly monitoring your behavior, seeking reassurance, or trying to perform belonging all reveal that belonging is unfamiliar to you.

### Part 3: Practical Application

**Exercise 1 - Example:**

Version A (Wrong): "Hi, I'm James Matthews, CEO of Thornton Industries. We just had our best year ever—doubled revenue. I graduated from Stanford, spent ten years at Goldman, and now we're the fastest-growing company in our sector. I'm also on the board of the Tech Foundation. Pleasure to meet you."

Version B (Right): "Hello, I'm James Matthews. A pleasure to meet you."

Key differences: Version A leads with title and achievements, name-drops, shares financial details, and lists credentials. Version B provides only what's needed—a name—and lets any further information emerge through conversation.

**Exercise 2 - Example Note:**

*Dear James and Elizabeth,*

*Thank you for a lovely evening. Your home is warm and welcoming, and the conversation at dinner was delightful. I particularly enjoyed our discussion of Florentine art—I'll have to revisit the Uffizi with fresh eyes after your insights about the Medici patronage.*

*With warm regards,*
*[Your name]*

Key elements: Handwritten. Thanks for hospitality. Mentions something specific. Brief. Warm but not effusive. No business requests.
