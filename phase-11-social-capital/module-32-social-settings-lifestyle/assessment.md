## Answer Key

### Part 1: Protocol Knowledge

**Question 1: C**
You should always know your giving amount before a paddle raise begins. Never attend a fundraising event without setting your budget in advance.

**Question 2: B**
Leave your phone in the car or locker room. Most exclusive clubs prohibit phones on the course. Guests don't tip (often prohibited), shouldn't bring their own clubs (host arranges), and never offer to pay.

**Question 3: B**
Stop bidding. Your maximum was $100,000 including premium. A $95,000 hammer price would exceed that with premium added. Auction discipline means honoring your limits.

**Question 4: C**
Never photograph someone's home interior for social media without explicit permission. Photos of private homes are inappropriate to share publicly.

**Question 5: B**
Give a brief, modest answer and redirect. Use language like "We were fortunate to find a good outcome" and then ask about them: "But tell me about your work."

**Question 6: B**
Always ask permission before posting photos with others. Even at charity events, individuals may not want to appear on social media. Venue interiors of private clubs are often restricted.

### Part 2: Scenario Analysis - Scoring Guide

**Scenario 1 (Museum Gala):**

Key points for full credit:
- **Preparation:** Research organization, know giving levels, set $5,000 budget, discuss with spouse if applicable
- **Cocktails:** Engage genuinely, ask questions, don't business-network aggressively
- **Dinner:** Draw out quieter tablemates, engage development director appropriately, show genuine interest in museum
- **Paddle raise:** Raise paddle confidently when $5,000 is called, don't hesitate, don't exceed budget
- **Follow-up:** Handwritten note to development director and/or table host within 48 hours

**Scenario 2 (Christie's Evening Sale):**

Key points for full credit:
- **Preparation:** Private viewing, condition report, provenance research, set firm maximum, register with paddle
- **During sale:** Stay calm, watch auctioneer rhythm, wait for your lot
- **At maximum:** Honor your limit, stop bidding, don't "go one more"
- **If lose:** Stay composed, congratulate winner graciously, don't show disappointment
- **Reception:** Stay, network graciously, don't dwell on loss, make connections for future
