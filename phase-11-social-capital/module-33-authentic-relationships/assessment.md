### Part 2: Scenario Analysis - Scoring Guide

**Scenario 1 (Wealth Transition with Friends):**

Key points for full credit:
- **Risks of paying:** Creating obligation, changing dynamic, making friends feel inadequate, signaling that things have changed
- **Risks of saying nothing:** Missing opportunity to be generous, seeming oblivious to differential
- **Approach:** Be genuine; perhaps offer to upgrade modestly and cover the differential, or suggest "I'd love to do something a bit nicer—let me cover the difference"; take cues from group
- **Principles:** Reciprocity doesn't require matching; honesty reduces awkwardness; focus on the relationship, not the transaction
- **Recommendation:** Something that acknowledges the change without overwhelming; follow group's comfort level; don't make it a big deal

**Scenario 2 (Building Old Money Relationship):**

Key points for full credit:
- **Year 1:** Continue showing up at shared events; deepen conversation about shared interests; don't mention business; focus on genuine connection
- **Year 2:** If appropriate, invite to something in your world (charity board you're on, event you're hosting); continue building relationship; still no direct business ask
- **Year 3:** If relationship has genuinely deepened, business might come up naturally; if he asks about your work, share briefly; let him indicate interest
- **Signals you're on track:** More personal invitations, introduction to family, longer conversations, he raises business topics
- **Mistakes to avoid:** Rushing, direct asks too early, name-dropping the relationship, social media mentions
