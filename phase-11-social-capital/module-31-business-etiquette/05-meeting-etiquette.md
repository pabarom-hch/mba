# Meeting Etiquette

## Learning Objectives

- Navigate formal boardroom meetings with confidence
- Understand private office visit protocols
- Manage informal meeting situations appropriately
- Master virtual meeting etiquette
- Handle difficult meeting dynamics gracefully

---

## Introduction

Meetings are where business happens—and where professional presence is constantly evaluated. From formal boardrooms to casual coffee meetings, each context has its protocols. Mastering meeting etiquette ensures you're remembered for your contributions, not your missteps.

---

## Core Concepts

### The Formal Boardroom

**Arrival:**
- Arrive 5-10 minutes early
- Announce yourself to reception
- Wait to be escorted to the room
- Don't sit until directed or until others are seated

**Seating:**
```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    BOARDROOM SEATING                             │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                                  │
│   HEAD: Host/Chair/Most senior person                           │
│                                                                  │
│   RIGHT OF HEAD: Guest of honor or second most senior           │
│                                                                  │
│   LEFT OF HEAD: Third most senior                               │
│                                                                  │
│   OPPOSITE HEAD: Second host or presenting party lead           │
│                                                                  │
│   If you're unsure: Wait to be directed or ask                  │
│                                                                  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**During the Meeting:**
- Silence phone completely (not vibrate)
- No checking phone during meeting
- Take notes on paper, not device (unless norm)
- Make eye contact when speaking and being spoken to
- Address the chair/most senior person appropriately
- Don't interrupt; wait for appropriate moments

**Materials:**
- Bring minimal materials (portfolio, pen, relevant documents)
- Don't spread papers across the table
- No food or drink unless offered
- Water is usually provided; okay to drink

### The Private Office Visit

**Entering Someone's Office:**

When visiting an executive in their office:
1. Arrive at reception area on time
2. Be seated if directed; stand if not
3. Wait to be escorted to office
4. Knock even if door is open
5. Wait to be invited in
6. Wait to be invited to sit

**Seating:**
- Host will indicate where to sit
- Usually guest chair(s) facing desk
- Don't move furniture
- Don't place items on their desk without asking

**The Environment:**
- Don't comment on personal items unless invited
- Don't pick up or touch objects
- Brief appreciation of office view or art is acceptable
- Focus should be on the person, not the space

**Phone Interruptions:**
- If host takes a call, look away, stay quiet
- Don't check your own phone
- Don't appear annoyed at interruption

### The Informal Meeting

**Coffee Meetings:**

The coffee meeting has its own protocol:
- Arrive first if possible
- Secure a table (not too isolated, not too public)
- When they arrive, stand to greet
- Order something (don't just have water)
- Keep to time unless they extend

**The "Walk-In":**

When someone says "do you have a minute?":
- Gauge actual availability honestly
- If not available: "I have a call in 10—can we schedule for later?"
- If available: Give full attention
- Don't multitask

**Standing Meetings:**

Quick conversations in hallways or at events:
- Make eye contact
- Don't position them awkwardly (back to crowd, etc.)
- Be aware if they're trying to exit
- Keep brief unless they engage deeper

### Virtual Meeting Etiquette

**Setup:**
- Professional background (or appropriate blur)
- Good lighting (face light, not backlit)
- Camera at eye level
- Quality audio (consider headset)
- Minimize visual clutter

**Behavior:**
- Mute when not speaking
- Look at camera when speaking (appears as eye contact)
- No multitasking (it's visible)
- Dress professionally (at least visible portion)
- Be aware of your facial expressions
- Allow for lag; don't interrupt

**The "Camera On" Norm:**
- When others have cameras on, yours should be too
- If you must be off, explain briefly
- In important meetings, camera on is expected

### Meeting Participation

**Speaking:**
- Don't monopolize
- Make substantive contributions
- Acknowledge others' points before adding
- Disagree respectfully
- Be concise

**Listening:**
- Active attention (nodding, eye contact)
- Take notes
- Don't side-talk
- Don't prepare your response while others speak

**Difficult Moments:**

| Situation | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Interrupted | "If I may finish..." then continue |
| Wrong name used | Correct gently once |
| Asked question you can't answer | "Let me verify and follow up" |
| Technical failure | Apologize briefly, solve quickly |
| Running over time | Check with group before continuing |

## CEO Application

### Running Meetings

As the leader:
- Start and end on time
- Set clear agenda and objectives
- Draw out quiet voices
- Keep dominant voices in check
- Summarize decisions and next steps
- Thank participants

### Being the Guest

When visiting another CEO or board:
- Extra attention to punctuality
- Higher deference to their protocols
- Follow their lead on formality
- Express appreciation for their time

### Board Meeting Dynamics

In board meetings:
- Address the chair appropriately
- Be prepared with materials
- Answer questions directly
- Don't surprise board members
- Follow up on commitments

## Worked Example

### Scenario

You're visiting the headquarters of a potential partner company for a meeting with their CEO and two VPs.

### The Approach

**Before:**
- Confirm meeting details day before
- Research attendees
- Prepare materials (minimal, professional)
- Plan to arrive 10 minutes early

**Arrival:**
- Check in with reception
- Wait patiently (don't check phone constantly)
- Stand when greeted
- Follow escort to meeting room

**The Meeting Room:**
- Wait to be seated or follow host's lead
- Place materials neatly in front of you
- Accept offered water/coffee
- Make small talk until meeting formally begins

**During Meeting:**
- Primary attention to CEO
- Include VPs in eye contact and dialogue
- Take notes
- Speak when appropriate, don't dominate
- Ask thoughtful questions

**Ending:**
- Don't overstay (watch for wrap-up cues)
- Thank each person
- Clear next steps
- Follow escort out

**After:**
- Thank you note to CEO within 48 hours
- Follow up on any commitments same day

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Phone Visibility
**Fix:** Phone completely away, on silent (not vibrate)

### Mistake 2: Arriving Late
**Fix:** Build in buffer; if unavoidably late, communicate and apologize once

### Mistake 3: Oversharing/Dominating
**Fix:** Contribute meaningfully but proportionately

### Mistake 4: Unprofessional Virtual Background
**Fix:** Use professional setting or appropriate blur

### Mistake 5: Not Following Up
**Fix:** Send follow-up same day or next morning

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Early is on time** - 5-10 minutes early for scheduled meetings

2. **Follow the host's lead** - Seating, formality, tone

3. **Phone away** - Not visible, not vibrating, not checked

4. **Contribute proportionately** - Speak meaningfully, don't dominate

5. **Virtual is still formal** - Camera on, professional background, no multitasking

6. **Follow up promptly** - Same day for action items, thank you within 48 hours
