## Answer Key

### Part 1: Protocol Knowledge

**Question 1: B**
The soup spoon is the outermost utensil on the right. Work from outside in.

**Question 2: B**
When being toasted, remain seated, make eye contact, acknowledge with a nod, but do not drink (you don't toast yourself).

**Question 3: B**
In Japan, business cards are presented with both hands, Japanese side facing the recipient, with a slight bow. This shows respect.

**Question 4: C**
At a private club, members only can pay, and tipping is often prohibited. Thank your host warmly and follow up with a handwritten note.

**Question 5: B**
Condolence notes should be brief, include a specific memory of the deceased if you knew them, and express sympathy. Avoid making it about you or making vague offers.

**Question 6: B**
When leaving the table temporarily, place your napkin loosely on your chair. On the table signals you're finished; on the plate is incorrect.

### Part 2: Scenario Analysis - Scoring Guide

**Scenario 1 (Hosting Business Dinner):**

Key points for full credit:
- **Preparation:** Make reservation in advance, arrive first, give credit card to maître d' discreetly
- **Arrival:** Greet guests standing, direct seating (honor guest to your right)
- **Wine:** Ask preferences, consult sommelier, taste and approve
- **Check:** Handle discreetly (excuse yourself or pre-arranged)
- **Follow-up:** Thank guests for their time, handwritten note to CEO

**Scenario 2 (Japan Meeting):**

Key points for full credit:
- **Cards:** Bilingual, present with both hands, study received cards, don't write on them
- **Greeting:** Bow (slight), exchange cards immediately after greeting
- **Meeting:** Patience, respect hierarchy, understand silence, don't expect immediate decisions
- **Gift:** Bring thoughtful gift, wrap beautifully, present with both hands
- **Follow-up:** Thank you communication, patience with response

### Part 3: Practical Application - Scoring Guide

**Exercise 1 (Thank-You Note):**

Strong note includes:
- Thanks both/all hosts
- References specific activity (golf) or conversation (Renaissance art)
- Mentions another couple or aspect of weekend
- Appropriate length (3-5 sentences)
- Warm but not effusive closing

Example:
*"Dear Margaret and William, Thank you for an extraordinary weekend at Willowbrook. Your hospitality was remarkable, and the company delightful. William, our conversation about the Medici patronage has sent me back to my Vasari—what a pleasure to share that interest. The Hendersons were charming dinner companions. With warm thanks, [Name]"*
