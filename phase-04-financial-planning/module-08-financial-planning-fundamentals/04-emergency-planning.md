# Emergency Planning

## Learning Objectives

- Determine appropriate emergency fund size
- Identify and plan for various emergency scenarios
- Structure emergency reserves for accessibility and returns
- Create contingency plans for income disruption
- Integrate emergency planning with overall strategy

---

## Introduction

Emergencies happen: job loss, health crises, car repairs, family needs. Without an emergency fund, these events force debt accumulation, investment liquidation at bad times, or worse. A well-structured emergency plan provides financial resilience and peace of mind.

---

## Core Concepts

### What Constitutes an Emergency?

```
True Emergencies:
✓ Job loss or income reduction
✓ Medical expenses not covered by insurance
✓ Major home repair (roof, HVAC, plumbing)
✓ Car repair when car is essential
✓ Family crisis requiring travel or support

NOT Emergencies:
✗ Vacation opportunities
✗ Sales on wanted items
✗ Routine maintenance (predictable)
✗ Holiday gifts (predictable)
✗ Regular car repairs (predictable)
```

### Emergency Fund Sizing

**Standard Guidelines:**

```
Income Stability:
- Stable salary, dual income: 3-4 months
- Single income, stable job: 6 months
- Variable income: 6-12 months
- Business owner/freelance: 12+ months

Job Market Factors:
- High-demand skills: Shorter
- Specialized/niche: Longer
- Senior level: Longer (harder to replace)
- Industry volatility: Longer

Family Factors:
- Dependents: Add 1-2 months
- Single parent: Add 2-3 months
- Special needs: Add appropriately
```

**Calculation Formula:**

```
Emergency Fund = Monthly Essential Expenses × Months Needed

Essential Expenses Include:
- Housing (mortgage/rent, taxes, insurance)
- Utilities
- Food (groceries only)
- Insurance premiums
- Minimum debt payments
- Transportation basics
- Healthcare

Essential Expenses Exclude:
- Discretionary spending
- Savings contributions
- Non-essential subscriptions
- Dining out
- Entertainment

Example:
Monthly essentials: $6,000
Stability factor: Single income, stable = 6 months
Emergency fund target: $36,000
```

### Emergency Fund Structure

**Three-Tier Approach:**

```
Tier 1: Immediate Access (Month 1)
- High-yield savings account
- Checking buffer
- Amount: 1 month expenses

Tier 2: Short-Term (Months 2-3)
- Money market account
- Short-term CDs (laddered)
- Amount: 2 months expenses

Tier 3: Extended (Months 4-6+)
- I-Bonds (after 1-year holding)
- CD ladder
- Conservative bond fund (taxable)
- Amount: Remaining months

Rationale: Optimize returns while maintaining access
```

### Where to Keep Emergency Funds

| Option | Accessibility | Return | Risk | Best For |
|--------|---------------|--------|------|----------|
| Checking | Immediate | 0% | None | Buffer only |
| HYSA | 1-2 days | 4-5% | None | Tier 1-2 |
| Money Market | 1-2 days | 4-5% | Very low | Tier 2 |
| Short CDs | Penalty | 4-5% | None | Tier 3 |
| I-Bonds | After 1 yr | Inflation | None | Tier 3 |
| Brokerage | 3-5 days | Variable | Market | Extended only |

## Beyond the Emergency Fund

### Insurance as Emergency Planning

```
Insurance transfers catastrophic risk:

Health Insurance:
- Max out-of-pocket: Include in emergency fund
- HSA: Can function as extended emergency

Disability Insurance:
- Short-term (STD): 60-90 days
- Long-term (LTD): After 90+ days
- Consider supplemental (60-70% of income)

Life Insurance:
- Replace income for dependents
- Rule of thumb: 10-12x income

Property Insurance:
- Homeowners/renters
- Auto
- Umbrella (for high earners)
```

### Income Disruption Planning

**Job Loss Scenario:**

```
Immediate Actions:
1. File for unemployment
2. Review severance terms
3. COBRA vs. marketplace insurance
4. Assess emergency fund adequacy

60-Day Plan:
1. Reduce expenses to essentials
2. Begin job search actively
3. Update resume and LinkedIn
4. Network intensively

90-Day Plan:
1. Consider temp/contract work
2. Evaluate geographic flexibility
3. Consider career pivot if needed
4. Review all financial obligations

Financial Bridges:
- Unemployment benefits
- Severance pay
- Consulting/freelance income
- Spouse income (if applicable)
```

### Contingency Planning Matrix

```
Scenario         | Probability | Impact | Mitigation
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Job loss         | Medium      | High   | Emergency fund, skills
Medical event    | Low-Med     | High   | Insurance, HSA
Car failure      | Medium      | Medium | Sinking fund, credit
Home repair      | Medium      | Medium | Sinking fund, HELOC
Market crash     | Low-Med     | High   | Diversification, time
Disability       | Low         | Very High | LTD insurance
Death of earner  | Low         | Very High | Life insurance
```

## CEO/Executive Considerations

### Golden Parachute Analysis

```
Typical Executive Protection:

Severance Package:
- 1-2 years base salary
- Prorated bonus
- Accelerated vesting
- COBRA coverage period
- Outplacement services

Change of Control:
- Additional payments
- Accelerated equity
- Modified terms

Review:
- Know your exact terms
- Understand trigger events
- Calculate total value
- Plan around timing
```

### Executive Emergency Fund Considerations

```
Higher Requirements:
- Longer job search (senior roles)
- Higher lifestyle expenses
- Tax implications larger
- Reputation management

Considerations:
- 12+ months recommended
- Include tax obligations
- Consider deferred comp access
- Line of credit as backup (not primary)

Alternative Liquidity:
- Pledged asset lines
- Securities-based lending
- Home equity line (in place, not drawn)
```

### Stock-Based Compensation as Emergency Reserve

```
Generally NOT Recommended:

Problems:
- Market timing risk
- Concentration risk
- Tax inefficiency
- Blackout periods
- Emotional selling

Better Approach:
- Systematic diversification over time
- Separate true emergency fund
- Equity for long-term, not emergencies

Exception:
- Portion of very large position
- As backup to primary emergency fund
- With tax-loss harvesting opportunity
```

## Building the Emergency Fund

### Starting from Zero

```
Phase 1: Starter Fund (Month 1-3)
Target: $1,000-$2,500
- Cover small emergencies
- Prevent credit card spirals
- Build momentum

Phase 2: One Month (Month 4-8)
Target: 1 month expenses
- Reduce anxiety
- Handle larger issues
- Continue building

Phase 3: Three Months (Month 9-18)
Target: 3 months expenses
- Adequate for most situations
- Job loss buffer

Phase 4: Full Fund (Month 19-36)
Target: 6 months expenses
- Complete peace of mind
- Weather any storm
```

### Acceleration Strategies

```
1. Tax Refund Allocation
   Allocate 50-100% to emergency fund

2. Bonus Deployment
   First priority until fully funded

3. Expense Reduction
   Temporary cuts → emergency fund

4. Side Income
   Dedicate entirely to fund building

5. Automatic Increases
   Increase transfer amount each month
```

### Maintaining the Fund

```
Replenishment Rules:

After Any Withdrawal:
1. Pause non-essential spending
2. Redirect savings to replenishment
3. Set timeline (typically 3-6 months)
4. Celebrate when restored

Annual Review:
1. Recalculate essential expenses
2. Adjust for life changes
3. Evaluate tier structure
4. Optimize returns within tiers
```

## Worked Example

### Emergency Plan Development

**Client Profile:**
- Household income: $180K
- Monthly expenses: $9,500
- Essential expenses: $6,200
- Current savings: $8,000
- Situation: Single income, two children

**Analysis:**

```
Recommended Emergency Fund:
- Stability: Single income = 6 months
- Dependents: +2 months = 8 months
- Target: $6,200 × 8 = $49,600

Current Status:
- Savings: $8,000
- Gap: $41,600
- Monthly savings capacity: $2,000

Building Plan:
- Phase 1 (Month 1-3): Reach $15,000
- Phase 2 (Month 4-12): Reach $33,000
- Phase 3 (Month 13-20): Reach $50,000
- Timeline: 20 months
```

**Structure:**

```
Tier 1 (Immediate): $6,200
- High-yield savings at Ally
- Linked to checking

Tier 2 (Month 2-4): $18,600
- Money market fund
- Fidelity Money Market

Tier 3 (Month 5-8): $24,800
- I-Bonds: $10,000
- 6-month CD ladder: $14,800
```

**Contingency Plan:**

```
If Job Loss Occurs:
1. Immediately file unemployment
2. Cut expenses to $5,000/month
3. Emergency fund lasts: ~10 months
4. Activate network immediately
5. Consider COBRA vs. ACA

Backup Options:
- Home equity line: $50K available (not drawn)
- Family loan: Possible $10K
- Retirement hardship: Last resort only
```

## Key Takeaways

1. **Emergency fund is non-negotiable** - Foundation of financial security
2. **Size depends on situation** - One size doesn't fit all
3. **Structure for access and return** - Tiered approach optimal
4. **Insurance complements fund** - Transfer catastrophic risk
5. **Plan for income disruption** - Know your scenarios
6. **Executives need more** - Higher stakes, longer searches
7. **Replenish immediately** - Treat as mandatory
