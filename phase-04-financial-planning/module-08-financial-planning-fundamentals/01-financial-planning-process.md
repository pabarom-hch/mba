# The Financial Planning Process

## Learning Objectives

- Understand the comprehensive financial planning framework
- Apply the CFP Board's 7-step process
- Identify data gathering requirements
- Set appropriate financial goals
- Develop integrated planning approaches

---

## Introduction

Financial planning is more than investing or budgeting—it's a comprehensive process that integrates all aspects of financial life into a coherent strategy. The CFP Board's financial planning process provides a systematic framework used by professionals worldwide.

For CEOs, this framework is particularly valuable: your financial situation is complex, your time is limited, and the stakes are high. A structured process ensures nothing falls through the cracks.

---

## Core Concepts

### The 7-Step Financial Planning Process

```
Step 1: Understanding the Client's Situation
        ↓
Step 2: Identifying and Selecting Goals
        ↓
Step 3: Analyzing Current Course and Alternatives
        ↓
Step 4: Developing Recommendations
        ↓
Step 5: Presenting Recommendations
        ↓
Step 6: Implementing Recommendations
        ↓
Step 7: Monitoring and Updating
        ↓
        [Return to Step 1 as needed]
```

### Step 1: Understanding the Situation

**Quantitative Data:**

```
Financial Statements:
├── Net worth statement
├── Cash flow statement
├── Tax returns (3 years)
└── Investment statements

Documents:
├── Insurance policies
├── Estate documents (will, trusts)
├── Employment contracts
├── Stock option agreements
├── Retirement plan statements
└── Debt agreements
```

**Qualitative Data:**

```
Personal Circumstances:
├── Family situation
├── Health considerations
├── Career trajectory
├── Risk tolerance
├── Values and priorities
└── Lifestyle expectations

Attitudes:
├── Money scripts and beliefs
├── Investment philosophy
├── Spending tendencies
└── Risk perception
```

### Step 2: Identifying Goals

**Goal Framework:**

```
SMART Financial Goals:

S - Specific: "Save for house down payment"
M - Measurable: "$100,000"
A - Achievable: Based on income and expenses
R - Relevant: Aligned with life priorities
T - Time-bound: "Within 3 years"
```

**Goal Categories:**

| Timeframe | Examples |
|-----------|----------|
| Short-term (< 1 year) | Emergency fund, debt payoff |
| Medium-term (1-5 years) | Home purchase, education fund |
| Long-term (5+ years) | Retirement, wealth building |
| Ongoing | Adequate insurance, estate planning |

**Goal Prioritization Matrix:**

```
                    High Impact
                         │
         Important &     │      Important &
         Not Urgent      │      Urgent
    (Retirement savings) │ (Emergency fund deficit)
                         │
Low Urgency ─────────────┼───────────────── High Urgency
                         │
         Not Important & │      Not Important &
         Not Urgent      │      Urgent
    (Vacation home)      │ (Market timing)
                         │
                    Low Impact
```

### Step 3: Analyzing Alternatives

**Current Course Analysis:**

```
Questions to Answer:

1. What happens if we do nothing?
   - Will current savings rate meet retirement?
   - Is insurance adequate if disaster strikes?
   - Will debt be paid before retirement?

2. What are the gaps?
   - Shortfall in retirement funding
   - Insurance coverage gaps
   - Estate planning deficiencies

3. What are the risks?
   - Market volatility impact
   - Job loss scenario
   - Health event impact
   - Premature death impact
```

**Alternative Analysis:**

```
For Each Goal, Consider:

Option A: Aggressive path
- Higher savings, lower current lifestyle
- More investment risk
- Faster timeline

Option B: Moderate path
- Balanced approach
- Moderate risk
- Standard timeline

Option C: Conservative path
- Lower savings requirement
- Reduced risk
- Extended timeline

Trade-offs: Risk vs. Return vs. Lifestyle
```

### Step 4: Developing Recommendations

**Recommendation Framework:**

```
Each Recommendation Should:

1. Address identified gap or opportunity
2. Align with client goals and values
3. Consider risk tolerance
4. Account for constraints (time, money, knowledge)
5. Integrate with other recommendations
6. Be actionable and specific

Format:
"We recommend [action] because [rationale],
which will [benefit], at a cost of [trade-off]."
```

**Integration Considerations:**

```
Cross-Functional Impact:

Investment Change:
├── Tax implications
├── Estate planning impact
├── Insurance needs change
└── Cash flow requirements

Insurance Change:
├── Estate plan updates
├── Investment allocation
├── Beneficiary coordination
└── Budget impact

Career Change:
├── Benefit review
├── Investment strategy
├── Risk profile update
└── Timeline adjustments
```

### Step 5: Presenting Recommendations

**Presentation Structure:**

```
1. Executive Summary
   - Key findings
   - Priority recommendations
   - Expected outcomes

2. Current Situation Analysis
   - Financial health assessment
   - Gap identification
   - Risk assessment

3. Recommendations by Area
   - Cash flow
   - Risk management
   - Investments
   - Tax planning
   - Retirement
   - Estate planning

4. Implementation Roadmap
   - Priority sequence
   - Timeline
   - Action items

5. Monitoring Plan
   - Review schedule
   - Key metrics
   - Update triggers
```

### Step 6: Implementation

**Implementation Checklist:**

```
Phase 1: Quick Wins (Month 1)
□ Update beneficiaries
□ Increase 401(k) contribution
□ Set up automatic savings
□ Cancel unnecessary subscriptions

Phase 2: Foundation (Months 2-3)
□ Build emergency fund
□ Review insurance coverage
□ Consolidate accounts
□ Set up investment accounts

Phase 3: Optimization (Months 4-6)
□ Implement investment strategy
□ Execute tax planning
□ Complete estate documents
□ Establish monitoring system
```

### Step 7: Monitoring and Updating

**Monitoring Framework:**

```
Regular Reviews:
├── Monthly: Cash flow, budget
├── Quarterly: Investment performance
├── Semi-annually: Insurance, goals
└── Annually: Comprehensive review

Trigger Events:
├── Marriage/divorce
├── Birth/adoption
├── Death in family
├── Job change
├── Inheritance
├── Major purchase
├── Health change
└── Market event (>20% change)
```

```
                    Cash Flow
                       │
       Risk ───────────┼─────────── Investments
      Management       │
           \          │           /
            \         │          /
             \        │         /
              ─────── Life ──────
             /    (Goals &   \
            /     Values)     \
           /         │         \
       Tax ──────────┼────────── Estate
      Planning       │         Planning
                     │
                 Retirement
```

### Integration Example

```
Goal: Retire at 55 with $3M

Cash Flow Impact:
- Required savings: $50K/year
- Budget restructuring needed

Investment Impact:
- Growth-oriented portfolio now
- Shift to income at retirement
- Tax-efficient placement

Risk Management:
- Disability insurance critical
- Life insurance needs assessment
- Long-term care consideration

Tax Planning:
- Maximize 401(k), backdoor Roth
- Tax-loss harvesting
- Retirement withdrawal sequencing

Estate Planning:
- Update beneficiaries
- Trust consideration

```

```
├── Section 16 reporting
├── Insider trading restrictions
```

## Worked Example

### Executive Financial Assessment

**Client Profile:**
- Age: 48, married, two children (16, 19)
- CEO of mid-size company
- Total compensation: $1.5M annually
- Net worth: $8M (70% company stock)

**Step 1: Situation Assessment**

```
Assets:
- Taxable investments: $1.2M
- 401(k): $800K
- Company stock (vested): $4.5M
- Stock options (vested): $1.5M
- Home: $1.2M
- Other: $300K

Liabilities:
- Mortgage: $500K
- HELOC: $100K

Net Worth: $8.9M

Issues Identified:
⚠️ 67% concentration in company
⚠️ Insufficient diversification
⚠️ Estate plan outdated
⚠️ Insurance gaps
```

**Step 2: Goal Identification**

```
Goals (Prioritized):
1. Retire at 58 ($200K/year lifestyle)
2. Fund children's education ($250K remaining)
3. Reduce company concentration to 30%
4. Establish charitable foundation
5. Update estate plan

Constraints:
- Blackout periods limit selling
- Emotional attachment to company stock
- Tax implications of large sales
```

**Step 3-4: Analysis and Recommendations**

```
Recommendation 1: Diversification
- Establish 10b5-1 plan to sell $500K/year
- Donate appreciated stock to DAF
- Exercise options systematically
- Target: 30% concentration by retirement

Recommendation 2: Retirement Funding
- Maximize 401(k) and catch-up
- Fund backdoor Roth IRA
- Create taxable investment portfolio
- Project: $12M by age 58

Recommendation 3: Risk Management
- Increase umbrella to $10M
- Add supplemental disability
- Review life insurance needs

Recommendation 4: Estate Planning
- Create revocable trust
- Update beneficiaries
- Consider GRAT for stock transfer
- Establish donor-advised fund
```

## Key Takeaways

1. **Process provides discipline** - Systematic approach ensures completeness
2. **Integration is essential** - Decisions affect multiple areas
3. **Goals drive strategy** - Everything stems from objectives
4. **Monitoring is ongoing** - Plans require regular updates
5. **Execution matters** - Best plan fails without implementation
6. **Complexity requires expertise** - Know when to get help
7. **Start where you are** - Perfect is enemy of good
