# Nexus: Live Business Lab

> Transform theoretical learning into applied business practice using real company data.

---

## Overview

The Nexus Live Business Lab connects the CEO Mentor curriculum to real business operations. Instead of studying hypothetical scenarios, you'll apply mentor principles to actual business challenges.

**Nexus** is a professional services platform managing:
- Sales pipeline using Miller Heiman methodology
- Financial performance and budgets
- Resource utilization and capacity
- Business turnaround and restructuring

Each quarter's mentor teachings are paired with Nexus exercises that make the lessons concrete and actionable.

---

## Quarterly Lab Exercises

### Q1: THINK (Weeks 1-13)
*Mental Models & Decision-Making with Munger and Buffett*

#### Lab Exercise: Multidisciplinary Financial Analysis

Using the Nexus financial dataset, apply Charlie Munger's mental models:

**Week 3 Exercise: Circle of Competence**
```
Review the Nexus business verticals:
- Government (58% of revenue)
- Healthcare (29% of revenue)
- Manufacturing (8% of revenue)
- Technology (5% of revenue)

Questions:
1. Which vertical represents the firm's circle of competence?
2. What data supports your answer?
3. Should the firm expand beyond this circle? Apply Munger's framework.
```

**Week 7 Exercise: Long-Term Thinking**
```
The Nexus turnaround case shows a company with 2 weeks of cash runway.

Apply Buffett's long-term thinking:
1. What decisions optimize for short-term survival?
2. What decisions optimize for long-term value?
3. How do you balance both?
4. What would Buffett sacrifice short-term?
```

**Week 10 Exercise: Margin of Safety**
```
Using the Nexus rate cards and utilization data:

1. Calculate the "margin of safety" in the current cost structure
2. What utilization rate provides a margin of safety?
3. How much cash reserve represents adequate margin of safety?
4. Apply this to your own business/career
```

### Q2: BUILD (Weeks 14-26)
*Wealth & Organizations with Carnegie and Hill*

#### Lab Exercise: Organizational Architecture

Using Nexus organizational data:

**Week 16 Exercise: Delegation & Scale (Carnegie)**
```
Nexus has 45 employees across multiple functions:
- Client Billable (32)
- Managed Services (6)
- Back Office (4)
- Management (3)

Questions:
1. Is this structure scalable? Why or why not?
2. What would Carnegie change to enable 10x growth?
3. How would you delegate authority?
4. Design an org chart for a $30M version of this company
```

**Week 20 Exercise: Mastermind Principle (Hill)**
```
The Nexus turnaround requires multiple stakeholders working together.

Apply Hill's Mastermind Principle:
1. Who should be in the turnaround "mastermind" group?
2. What does each member contribute?
3. How do you align their interests?
4. Design a weekly mastermind meeting agenda
```

**Week 24 Exercise: Definiteness of Purpose (Hill)**
```
Review the Nexus strategic repositioning:

From: "We provide IT staffing through our primary client"
To: "We help states modernize their public health data infrastructure"

Questions:
1. Which statement reflects "definiteness of purpose"?
2. How does purpose clarity affect sales, hiring, and strategy?
3. Write a purpose statement for your own career/business
4. How would this change your daily decisions?
```

### Q3: BE (Weeks 27-39)
*Personal Mastery & Character with Rohn and Gandhi*

#### Lab Exercise: Daily Discipline & Moral Leadership

Using Nexus operational data:

**Week 29 Exercise: The Daily Disciplines (Rohn)**
```
The Nexus platform tracks daily activities, checklists, and notes.

Design your own CEO "daily dozen" based on Rohn's principles:
1. What 12 activities, done daily, would transform your effectiveness?
2. How would you track them?
3. What's the compound effect over 1 year?
4. Apply this to the Nexus business - what 12 daily habits would turn it around?
```

**Week 34 Exercise: Leading by Example (Gandhi)**
```
The Nexus employee stakeholder group is nervous about job security.

Apply Gandhi's principles:
1. What does "be the change" mean for a CEO in crisis?
2. What personal sacrifices demonstrate commitment?
3. How do you maintain moral authority while making difficult decisions?
4. Draft an employee communication that embodies these principles
```

**Week 38 Exercise: Service & Sacrifice (Gandhi)**
```
Review the Nexus stakeholder analysis:
- Employees want job security
- Bank wants repayment
- Client wants continuity
- Ownership wants return

Questions:
1. What does servant leadership look like here?
2. Whose interests should come first?
3. How do you serve all stakeholders?
4. What would Gandhi prioritize?
```

### Q4: LEAD (Weeks 40-52)
*Leadership & Legacy with Mandela and Maxwell*

#### Lab Exercise: Crisis Leadership & Influence

Using Nexus turnaround and stakeholder data:

**Week 42 Exercise: Leading Through Adversity (Mandela)**
```
The Nexus turnaround case represents a business in crisis.

Apply Mandela's leadership principles:
1. How do you maintain hope while being honest about challenges?
2. What role does forgiveness play in turnaround leadership?
3. How do you unite former adversaries?
4. Write a "turnaround vision" speech inspired by Mandela
```

**Week 46 Exercise: The Law of Influence (Maxwell)**
```
You need to influence multiple stakeholders without direct authority:
- Bank (controls credit)
- Client (controls revenue)
- Employees (control execution)

Apply Maxwell's 5 Levels of Leadership:
1. Where is your influence with each group?
2. How do you move to the next level?
3. What specific actions increase influence?
4. Create an influence development plan
```

**Week 50 Exercise: Developing Leaders (Maxwell)**
```
The Nexus business needs to build leadership capacity.

Questions:
1. Who are the emerging leaders in the organization?
2. How do you develop them during a crisis?
3. What leadership responsibilities can you delegate?
4. Design a 90-day leader development plan
```

## Using This Lab

### Integration with Daily Practice

1. **Morning Ritual**: Read the day's mentor principle, then review related Nexus data
2. **Midday Application**: Apply the principle to a real business decision
3. **Evening Reflection**: Journal about what you learned from the application

### Weekly Exercises

Each week includes:
- A specific Nexus dataset to analyze
- 3-4 application questions
- A connection to the mentor's core teaching
- A personal application prompt

### Quarterly Projects

At the end of each quarter, complete a capstone project:
- Q1: Financial analysis memo using Munger/Buffett frameworks
- Q2: Organizational design proposal using Carnegie/Hill principles
- Q3: Personal development plan using Rohn/Gandhi practices
- Q4: Leadership case study using Mandela/Maxwell teachings
