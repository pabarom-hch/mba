# Case Study: Stakeholder Negotiation in Crisis

## Case Overview

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Company | TechServices Corp (Professional Services Firm) |
| Industry | IT Consulting & Staffing |
| Revenue | ~$3.2M annually (declining) |
| Challenge | Negotiate with multiple stakeholders during business crisis |
| Time Limit | 50 minutes (individual) or 90 minutes (group/roleplay) |
| Difficulty | Advanced (Connects to Phase 8-9: Leadership) |

---

## Background

TechServices Corp is in crisis with only 2 weeks of cash runway. As the turnaround advisor, you must negotiate with five key stakeholder groups simultaneously, each with different interests and leverage:

1. **Employees** - Concerned about job security
2. **Bank** - Holds line of credit, wants repayment assurance
3. **Primary Client** - Largest revenue source, relationship at risk
4. **Ownership** - Wants business viability and return
5. **Vendors** - Owed money, want payment

Your goal is to secure agreements that stabilize the business while maintaining relationships for the long term.

## Exhibit 3: Negotiation Scenarios

### Scenario A: Employee Town Hall

You must address all 45 employees about the company's situation.

**Context:**
- Rumors are circulating about cash problems
- Morale is declining
- Key talent is being recruited by competitors
- Next payroll is in 10 days

**Questions to Address:**
1. What is happening with the company?
2. Is my job safe?
3. Will we get paid on time?
4. What's the plan going forward?

**Your Goal:**
- Maintain trust and morale
- Retain key talent
- Be honest without creating panic
- Rally team around turnaround

---

### Scenario B: Bank Meeting

You need to draw on the line of credit and may need covenant relief.

**Context:**
- $150,000 available
- Need to draw $100,000 immediately
- May breach covenant next quarter
- Relationship manager is supportive but needs approval

**Questions from Bank:**
1. Why do you need the draw?
2. What's your repayment plan?
3. Will you maintain covenant compliance?
4. What collateral/guarantees can you offer?

**Your Goal:**
- Secure immediate draw
- Obtain covenant waiver (if needed)
- Maintain banking relationship
- Avoid personal guarantee (if possible)

---

### Scenario C: Client Conversation

You need to understand the revenue drop and secure commitment.

**Context:**
- Revenue dropped 46% last month
- You don't know why
- This client represents 95% of revenue
- They haven't raised concerns

**Questions to Explore:**
1. Is the reduction permanent or temporary?
2. Are they satisfied with our service?
3. Are there opportunities to expand?
4. What concerns do they have about us?

**Your Goal:**
- Understand root cause
- Rebuild/strengthen relationship
- Identify expansion opportunities
- Secure commitment for next 6 months

## Exhibit 4: Negotiation Frameworks

### Fisher/Ury Principled Negotiation

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| Separate People from Problem | Address relationship concerns separately from business issues |
| Focus on Interests, Not Positions | Understand WHY each stakeholder wants what they want |
| Generate Options for Mutual Gain | Create solutions that help multiple parties |
| Use Objective Criteria | Base agreements on fair standards, precedent, market rates |

### BATNA Analysis

| Stakeholder | Their BATNA | Your BATNA | Balance of Power |
|-------------|-------------|------------|------------------|
| Employees | Find new jobs | Replace (difficult) | Balanced |
| Bank | Call loan (lose $) | Other lenders (difficult) | Bank slightly |
| Client | Other vendors | Other clients (long term) | Client strongly |
| Ownership | Sell/close (lose $) | Other investors (difficult) | Ownership |
| Vendors | Sue/collections | Other vendors | You slightly |

## Discussion Questions

### Question 1: Stakeholder Mapping (10 minutes)

Create a comprehensive stakeholder map:
1. Rate each stakeholder's power (1-5) and interest (1-5)
2. Identify who can help vs. who can hurt the turnaround
3. Determine the optimal sequence for negotiations
4. What stakeholders are you missing?

### Question 2: Employee Communication (10 minutes)

Draft talking points for the employee town hall:
1. How do you explain the situation honestly without creating panic?
2. What commitments can you make vs. what must remain uncertain?
3. How do you handle the question "Is my job safe?"
4. What tone should you strike?

### Question 3: Bank Negotiation (15 minutes)

Prepare for the bank meeting:
1. What is your opening ask?
2. What are you willing to offer in return?
3. What is your walk-away point?
4. How do you handle the covenant conversation?
5. Role-play the first 5 minutes of this conversation

### Question 4: Client Discovery (10 minutes)

Design your approach to the primary client conversation:
1. What questions will reveal the root cause of the revenue drop?
2. How do you position the conversation (curious vs. concerned)?
3. What do you share about your situation?
4. What is your ask at the end of the meeting?

### Question 5: Integrated Stakeholder Strategy (15 minutes)

Design a stakeholder management plan for the next 30 days:
1. Create a communication calendar (who, when, what)
2. Identify dependencies between stakeholder agreements
3. What happens if one negotiation fails?
4. How do you maintain trust with all parties?

## Consulting Approach

### Crisis Communication Framework

```
ACKNOWLEDGE → ASSURE → ACT → ALIGN

1. ACKNOWLEDGE the situation
   - Be honest about challenges
   - Don't sugarcoat or catastrophize
   - Show you understand concerns

2. ASSURE stakeholders
   - Immediate actions being taken
   - Leadership is engaged
   - Their interests are considered

3. ACT with specific plans
   - Concrete next steps
   - Timeline and milestones
   - Accountability

4. ALIGN for the future
   - Shared vision for recovery
   - Their role in success
   - Regular communication cadence
```

### Negotiation Preparation Checklist

- [ ] Know your BATNA
- [ ] Research their BATNA
- [ ] Identify their interests (not just positions)
- [ ] Prepare opening and fallback positions
- [ ] Plan your concession strategy
- [ ] Anticipate objections
- [ ] Define success criteria
- [ ] Plan follow-up actions

## Teaching Note

### Key Learning Objectives

1. **Stakeholder analysis** - Map power, interest, and influence
2. **Principled negotiation** - Focus on interests, not positions
3. **Crisis communication** - Honest, clear, action-oriented
4. **Multi-party dynamics** - Manage interconnected negotiations
5. **Leadership presence** - Maintain composure under pressure

### Role-Play Recommendations

This case works best as a multi-party negotiation exercise:
- Assign students to stakeholder groups
- Run simultaneous negotiations
- Debrief on dynamics and outcomes
- Discuss what worked and what didn't
