## Strategic Sourcing

### The Procurement Function

```
PROCUREMENT EVOLUTION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

TRADITIONAL (Tactical):              STRATEGIC:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"Buying stuff"                       "Managing supply base"

• Order placement                    • Category strategy
• Price negotiation                  • Supplier development
• Transaction processing             • Risk management
• Reactive to requests               • Value creation

Cost center                          Value contributor


PROCUREMENT'S IMPACT:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Typical company:
Revenue:         $100M
Purchased goods:  $60M (60% of revenue)
Operating profit: $10M (10% margin)

SCENARIO: Reduce purchasing costs by 5%

Savings: $60M × 5% = $3M
New profit: $13M (30% increase!)

To achieve same profit through sales:
Need $30M additional revenue (30% growth)

LEVERAGE: Procurement savings have higher profit impact
than equivalent sales increase
```

```
KRALJIC MATRIX:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Classification based on:
• Supply Risk (complexity, availability)
• Profit Impact (spend, value)

              Supply Risk
            Low        High
         ┌──────────┬──────────┐
    High │LEVERAGE  │STRATEGIC │
Profit   │          │          │
Impact   │Exploit   │Partner   │
         │power     │closely   │
         ├──────────┼──────────┤
    Low  │NON-      │BOTTLENECK│
         │CRITICAL  │          │
         │Simplify  │Secure    │
         │          │supply    │
         └──────────┴──────────┘


STRATEGIES BY QUADRANT:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

NON-CRITICAL (Low impact, Low risk):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Minimize transaction costs
• Automate ordering
• Use catalogs, P-cards
• Consolidate suppliers
Examples: Office supplies, MRO items

LEVERAGE (High impact, Low risk):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Use purchasing power
• Competitive bidding
• Short-term contracts
• Maximize cost savings
Examples: Packaging, standard components

BOTTLENECK (Low impact, High risk):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Ensure supply availability
• Safety stock
• Develop alternatives
• Accept higher cost for reliability
Examples: Specialty chemicals, unique parts

STRATEGIC (High impact, High risk):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Collaborative relationship
• Joint development
• Long-term partnership
• Risk/reward sharing
Examples: Critical technology, key materials
```

## Supplier Selection

### Evaluation Criteria

```
SUPPLIER EVALUATION FRAMEWORK:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

QUALITY (25-35% weight typically):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Defect rates (PPM)
• Quality certifications (ISO, industry)
• Quality management systems
• Continuous improvement capability
• Warranty and returns

COST (20-30% weight):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Unit price
• Total cost of ownership
• Payment terms
• Price stability
• Cost reduction programs

DELIVERY (15-25% weight):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• On-time delivery rate
• Lead time reliability
• Flexibility (expedite capability)
• Order accuracy
• Communication/visibility

SERVICE (10-20% weight):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Responsiveness
• Technical support
• Problem resolution
• Account management
• Proactive communication

CAPABILITY (10-15% weight):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Financial stability
• Capacity and scalability
• Technology and innovation
• Geographic footprint
• Management quality

SUSTAINABILITY (5-15% weight, growing):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Environmental practices
• Labor standards
• Supply chain transparency
• ESG compliance
• Certifications
```

## Total Cost of Ownership

### TCO Framework

```
TOTAL COST OF OWNERSHIP:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

TCO captures ALL costs of acquiring and using an item

                    ┌─────────────────┐
                    │  PURCHASE PRICE │ ← Visible
                    │    (Tip of      │   (30-50%)
                    │    iceberg)     │
                ────┴─────────────────┴────
             ╱                              ╲
            ╱  HIDDEN COSTS                  ╲
           │   • Freight, duties             │
           │   • Inspection, testing         │
           │   • Inventory carrying          │
           │   • Quality failures            │
           │   • Administrative              │
           │   • Supplier management         │
           │   • Risk and disruption         │
            ╲                               ╱ (50-70%)
             ╲─────────────────────────────╱


TCO COST CATEGORIES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PRE-TRANSACTION COSTS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Supplier identification and qualification
• RFQ/RFP process
• Contract negotiation
• Supplier visits and audits

TRANSACTION COSTS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Purchase price
• Transportation and logistics
• Duties and tariffs
• Payment processing
• Receiving and inspection

POST-TRANSACTION COSTS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Defects and rework
• Warranty claims
• Returns processing
• Inventory carrying
• Stock-outs (lost sales)
• Supplier support needed
```

### TCO Calculation Example

```
TCO COMPARISON EXAMPLE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Comparing two suppliers for electronic component
Annual volume: 100,000 units

                         Supplier A    Supplier B
                         (Domestic)    (Offshore)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Unit Price               $10.00        $7.50

Purchase Cost            $1,000,000    $750,000

Transportation:
 - Freight               $30,000       $80,000
 - Duties (5%)           $0            $37,500
 - Insurance             $5,000        $15,000

Quality:
 - Incoming inspection   $10,000       $25,000
 - Defect rate (cost)    $20,000       $45,000
   (1% vs 2% @ $200/def)

Inventory:
 - Lead time (2 vs 8 wk) $15,000       $60,000
   carrying cost
 - Safety stock          $10,000       $40,000

Administrative:
 - Order processing      $5,000        $5,000
 - Supplier management   $10,000       $25,000
 - Travel/audit          $5,000        $20,000

Risk:
 - Disruption reserve    $20,000       $50,000

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
TOTAL TCO               $1,130,000    $1,152,500

TCO per unit             $11.30        $11.53
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Despite 25% lower purchase price, offshore supplier
has HIGHER total cost of ownership!
```

### Contract Management

```
CONTRACT TYPES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FIXED PRICE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Price set for contract term
• Supplier bears cost risk
• Best for: Stable, well-defined requirements

COST-PLUS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Buyer pays cost + margin
• Buyer bears cost risk
• Best for: Uncertain scope, development work

FIXED PRICE WITH ESCALATION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Price adjusts with index (material, labor)
• Shared risk
• Best for: Long-term, commodity-linked

VOLUME-TIERED:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Price decreases with volume
• Encourages commitment
• Best for: Growing demand scenarios


KEY CONTRACT ELEMENTS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

□ Pricing and payment terms
□ Volume commitments (min/max)
□ Quality requirements and remedies
□ Delivery terms (Incoterms)
□ Lead time requirements
□ Intellectual property
□ Confidentiality
□ Liability and indemnification
□ Term and termination
□ Change management process
□ Dispute resolution
□ Force majeure


INCOTERMS (Common):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

EXW (Ex Works):     Buyer arranges all transport
FOB (Free on Board): Seller delivers to ship
CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight): Seller pays to destination
DDP (Delivered Duty Paid): Seller bears all costs/risk

Risk transfer point determines who pays if goods damaged
```

## Key Takeaways

1. **Procurement is strategic** - 5% savings can equal 30% profit increase
2. **Categorize spend strategically** - Kraljic matrix guides approach by category
3. **TCO > Purchase price** - Hidden costs often 50-70% of total cost
4. **Develop strategic suppliers** - Investment in key suppliers pays off
5. **Contracts manage risk** - Right structure aligns incentives
6. **Sustainability matters** - ESG increasingly important to stakeholders
