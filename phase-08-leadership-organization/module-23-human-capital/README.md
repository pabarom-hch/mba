# Module 23: Human Capital Management

## Phase 8: Leadership & Organization

---

## Module Overview

Human Capital Management (HCM) has evolved from administrative personnel management to a strategic driver of competitive advantage. This module examines how organizations attract, develop, engage, and retain talent to achieve strategic objectives. You will learn to design HR systems that build organizational capability and create sustainable value through people.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

1. **Align talent strategy with business strategy** to build required organizational capabilities
2. **Design effective talent acquisition systems** including employer branding and selection
3. **Build performance management systems** that drive results and development
4. **Create total rewards strategies** that attract, motivate, and retain talent
5. **Develop learning and career development programs** that build capabilities
6. **Implement succession planning processes** that ensure leadership continuity
7. **Measure and improve employee engagement** to drive retention and productivity
8. **Apply HR analytics** to make evidence-based people decisions

## Study Approach

### Recommended Sequence

1. **Start with Talent Strategy** to understand the strategic context
2. **Study Performance Management** to see how goals cascade
3. **Examine Compensation & Rewards** to understand motivation systems
4. **Explore Learning & Development** for capability building
5. **Review Succession Planning** for leadership continuity
6. **Conclude with Employee Engagement** for retention drivers
7. **Complete assessment** and case studies to apply learning

### Preparation for Assessment

- Review all key frameworks and their applications
- Practice calculating and interpreting HR metrics
- Be prepared to design HR programs for specific situations
- Understand trade-offs in HR system design

---

## Key Takeaways Preview

1. **HR is strategic**: People practices directly impact business outcomes
2. **Systems thinking**: HR practices work together as a system
3. **Employee experience**: The cumulative effect of all interactions
4. **Data-driven**: Metrics and analytics enable better decisions
5. **Context matters**: No one-size-fits-all approach to HR
6. **Continuous evolution**: HR must adapt to changing work
