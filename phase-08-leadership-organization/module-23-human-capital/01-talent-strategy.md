### Build vs. Buy vs. Borrow vs. Bot

| Strategy | Description | When to Use | Considerations |
|----------|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| **BUILD** | Develop talent internally | Core, long-term capabilities | Time required; retention risk |
| **BUY** | Hire externally | Urgent needs; new capabilities | Cost; cultural fit; availability |
| **BORROW** | Contractors, consultants, gig | Temporary needs; specialized skills | Knowledge retention; management |
| **BOT** | Automate with technology | Routine, rule-based tasks | Investment; change management |

### Sourcing Channels

| Channel | Best For | Pros | Cons |
|---------|----------|------|------|
| **Job Boards** | Volume roles | Wide reach; cost effective | High volume, low quality |
| **LinkedIn** | Professional roles | Targeted; passive candidates | Expensive; competitive |
| **Referrals** | Culture fit | Quality; speed; retention | Limited diversity; bias |
| **Campus** | Entry-level | Pipeline building; fresh talent | Seasonal; training required |
| **Agencies** | Senior/specialized | Speed; expertise | Cost (20-30% of salary) |
| **Internal** | Retention | Morale; known quantity | Succession gaps created |

### Candidate Experience Best Practices

1. **Respond quickly**: Acknowledge applications within 24 hours
2. **Communicate status**: Provide regular updates throughout process
3. **Respect time**: Be punctual; don't over-interview
4. **Prepare interviewers**: Train them on process and candidate info
5. **Provide feedback**: Give constructive feedback to rejected candidates
6. **Close the loop**: Every candidate should receive a final response

## Practice Exercise: Talent Strategy Design

### Scenario

You are the Chief People Officer at a mid-sized financial services company ($500M revenue, 2,000 employees) that is embarking on a digital transformation. The CEO has announced a strategic shift to become a "digital-first" company within three years.

**Current State:**
- Average employee age: 45
- 80% of workforce has been with company >10 years
- Digital skills limited primarily to IT department
- Located primarily in one geographic region
- Turnover: 5% (low but concerning for fresh ideas)

**Future State Requirements:**
- Need 300+ employees with digital skills (data science, cloud, UX)
- Want to attract younger, digitally-native talent
- Considering remote work to access wider talent pools
- Must maintain core business while transforming

### Task

Develop a comprehensive talent strategy addressing:

1. **Workforce Planning Analysis**
   - What is your assessment of the current workforce vs. future needs?
   - Which roles are critical to the transformation?
   - What is your build/buy/borrow/bot recommendation?

2. **Employer Brand Strategy**
   - How will you position the company to attract digital talent?
   - What EVP elements need to change?
   - What proof points can you create?

3. **Talent Acquisition Plan**
   - What sourcing channels will you prioritize?
   - How will you assess for digital skills and transformation mindset?
   - What timeline and budget do you propose?

4. **Risk Mitigation**
   - How will you maintain engagement of existing workforce?
   - What retention strategies for critical legacy knowledge?
   - How will you manage culture integration?
