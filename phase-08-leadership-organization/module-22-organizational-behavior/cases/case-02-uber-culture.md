## Case Background

Uber became one of the fastest-growing startups in history, reaching a valuation of $70 billion by 2017. The company revolutionized urban transportation and became synonymous with the gig economy. However, behind the meteoric growth lurked a toxic corporate culture that would eventually force the departure of the CEO and fundamentally reshape the company.

In February 2017, former Uber engineer Susan Fowler published a blog post titled "Reflecting on One Very, Very Strange Year at Uber" that exposed systematic sexual harassment, discrimination, and a toxic workplace culture. This single blog post triggered a cascade of revelations that would become one of the most significant corporate culture crises in Silicon Valley history.

### Susan Fowler's Revelations

Key issues documented in Fowler's account:

1. **Sexual harassment by manager**: On her first day, her manager propositioned her via chat
2. **HR failure**: When reported, HR declined to act because it was the manager's "first offense"
3. **Retaliation**: Promised transfer blocked; received negative performance review
4. **Pattern of behavior**: Discovered same manager had harassed multiple women
5. **Organizational gaslighting**: HR suggested she might be the problem
6. **Systemic nature**: Described multiple incidents across the organization

### Five Dysfunctions Analysis

Applying Lencioni's framework to Uber:

| Dysfunction | Evidence at Uber |
|-------------|------------------|
| **Absence of Trust** | Fear of retaliation; hiding problems; no psychological safety |
| **Fear of Conflict** | Ironically, lots of conflict but not productive; conflict was personal, not about ideas |
| **Lack of Commitment** | Unclear ethical boundaries; inconsistent standards applied |
| **Avoidance of Accountability** | High performers exempt; HR refused to act |
| **Inattention to Results** | Individual "winning" prioritized over company reputation and sustainability |

## The Holder Report

Former U.S. Attorney General Eric Holder was hired to investigate. Key findings and recommendations:

### Critical Findings

1. **HR ineffectiveness**: Human Resources failed to properly handle complaints
2. **Leadership failures**: Senior leadership set poor tone and example
3. **Value misinterpretation**: Cultural values were used to justify inappropriate behavior
4. **Accountability gaps**: No consistent enforcement of policies
5. **Retaliation**: Employees feared speaking up

## Aftermath and Current State

### Progress Made

- **IPO achieved**: Uber went public in May 2019
- **Improved reputation**: Glassdoor ratings improved
- **Regulatory relationships**: Better relations with cities and regulators
- **Diversity progress**: Slow but measurable improvement in diversity metrics
- **Compliance infrastructure**: Robust systems now in place

### Ongoing Challenges

- **Driver relations**: Classification and treatment issues persist
- **Profitability**: Continued losses despite revenue growth
- **Competition**: Facing strong competition globally
- **Cultural legacy**: Some remnants of old culture remain
- **External perception**: Reputation still recovering

## Assignment: Culture Crisis Response Plan

### Scenario

You are the newly appointed Chief People Officer at a fast-growing technology company. A blog post has just been published by a former employee detailing harassment, discrimination, and a toxic work environment. The post is going viral, and media outlets are calling for comment.

### Part 1: Immediate Response (24-48 hours) - 30%

Develop an immediate response plan:
1. What communications do you issue (internal and external)?
2. Who do you involve in the response?
3. What actions do you take regarding the accused individuals?
4. How do you protect the company legally while acknowledging the problem?

### Part 2: Investigation & Diagnosis (30 days) - 30%

Design an investigation plan:
1. Who should lead the investigation (internal, external, or both)?
2. What questions should the investigation answer?
3. How will you protect employees who come forward?
4. What data and documentation will you need?

### Part 3: Culture Transformation (6-12 months) - 40%

Develop a comprehensive culture transformation plan:
1. What structural changes are needed (governance, HR, policies)?
2. How will you redefine cultural values?
3. What systems will measure and reinforce the new culture?
4. How will you know if the transformation is successful?

Include specific timelines, responsible parties, and success metrics.
