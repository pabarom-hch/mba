## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

1. **Define** executive presence and its three pillars
2. **Assess** your own executive presence strengths and gaps
3. **Develop** gravitas through confidence and decisiveness
4. **Enhance** communication impact through voice and language
5. **Project** appropriate professional appearance and demeanor

---

## Introduction

Executive presence is the ability to inspire confidence in others that you are capable of leading. It's the "it factor" that distinguishes those who are seen as leadership material from those who aren't. Research by the Center for Talent Innovation found that executive presence accounts for 26% of what it takes to get promoted to senior leadership.

## 5. Context-Specific Presence

### Presence in Different Settings

| Setting | Key Presence Elements | Common Pitfalls |
|---------|----------------------|-----------------|
| Board Presentations | Concise, confident, data-driven | Over-explaining, nervous body language |
| Team Meetings | Authoritative yet inclusive | Dominating or being too passive |
| Crisis Situations | Calm, decisive, reassuring | Showing panic, over-reacting |
| Negotiations | Firm but fair, poker face | Revealing too much, backing down easily |
| One-on-Ones | Engaged, direct, empathetic | Distracted, too formal or too casual |
| Virtual Meetings | Eye contact with camera, energy | Looking away, monotone, multitasking |

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: Voice Recording

Record yourself giving a 2-minute briefing on a topic you know well:

1. Listen for filler words (um, uh, like, you know)
2. Note your pace (too fast? too slow? varied?)
3. Assess your tone (confident? uncertain? monotone?)
4. Identify one area to improve and re-record

### Exercise 2: Body Language Audit

In your next meeting:

1. Note your posture at the start, middle, and end
2. Count how many times you made eye contact with each person
3. Observe any nervous habits or fidgeting
4. Rate your spatial presence (did you take appropriate space?)

### Exercise 3: Language Upgrade

Review your recent emails and identify:

1. Weak phrases that could be stronger
2. Unnecessary qualifiers (just, sort of, kind of)
3. Passive voice that could be active
4. Rewrite three sentences with stronger executive language
