## Case Overview

You have been appointed CEO of Meridian Manufacturing, a mid-sized industrial equipment manufacturer facing severe financial distress. This comprehensive case requires you to integrate knowledge from all MBA disciplines to lead a successful turnaround.

**Time to Complete**: 3-4 hours
**Difficulty**: Advanced
**Integration Requirement**: All nine phases of MBA curriculum

## Case Questions

### Part A: Immediate Crisis Management (Apply Phase 2, 8 knowledge)

1. **Cash Management**
   - What immediate actions would you take in the first two weeks to stabilize cash?
   - How would you approach the covenant violation with the senior lenders?
   - What assets could be monetized quickly without damaging the business?

2. **Stakeholder Communication**
   - Draft your Day 1 message to employees
   - What would you tell the three major customers who have requested meetings?
   - How would you handle media inquiries about the company's financial health?

### Part B: Strategic Assessment (Apply Phase 1, 3, 4 knowledge)

3. **Market Analysis**
   - Based on the competitive analysis, what is Meridian's sustainable competitive advantage, if any?
   - Which business (Heavy Machinery, Precision Components, or Service) offers the best path to recovery?
   - Should Meridian exit any of its businesses? Which one(s) and why?

4. **Customer and Revenue**
   - How would you prioritize customer retention efforts?
   - What pricing actions would you consider?
   - What is your assessment of the Service business as a turnaround lever?

### Part C: Operational Turnaround (Apply Phase 5 knowledge)

5. **Cost Reduction**
   - Develop a detailed cost reduction plan targeting $50M in annual savings
   - Which plants would you consolidate or close? Justify your decision
   - How would you address the inventory problem ($25M excess/obsolete)?

6. **Operations Improvement**
   - What operational initiatives would improve margins beyond cost cutting?
   - How would you address the quality problems?
   - What quick wins could improve working capital?

### Part D: People and Organization (Apply Phase 6, 9 knowledge)

7. **Leadership Team**
   - What criteria would you use to assess the current executive team?
   - What kind of CFO should you hire for this situation?
   - How would you address the engineering talent exodus?

8. **Labor Relations**
   - How would you approach the upcoming union contract negotiation?
   - What would you offer the union in exchange for cost concessions?
   - How would you address the pension underfunding?

9. **Culture Change**
   - What cultural shifts are needed for the turnaround to succeed?
   - How would you create urgency without destroying morale?
   - How would you maintain trust while making difficult decisions?

### Part E: Financial Restructuring (Apply Phase 2, 7 knowledge)

10. **Capital Structure**
    - What financial restructuring options are available?
    - How would you approach the subordinated note holders?
    - Is an out-of-court restructuring possible? What would it require?

11. **Valuation**
    - What is Meridian worth in its current state?
    - What could it be worth after a successful turnaround?
    - How would you value the individual business units for potential divestiture?

### Part F: Integrated Turnaround Plan (Apply all phases)

12. **100-Day Plan**
    - Create a detailed 100-day action plan for the turnaround
    - What are your top 5 priorities and why?
    - What metrics will you track to measure progress?

13. **Three-Year Roadmap**
    - Develop a three-year financial projection showing path to recovery
    - What strategic position should Meridian target by Year 3?
    - What are the key risks to the plan and how would you mitigate them?

14. **Board Presentation**
    - Prepare an executive summary of your turnaround plan for the board
    - What tough trade-offs will you recommend?
    - What support do you need from the board?
