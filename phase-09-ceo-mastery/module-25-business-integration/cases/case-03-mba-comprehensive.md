## Case Overview

This comprehensive capstone case requires you to apply the full breadth of your MBA learning to a complex, multi-dimensional business challenge. As the newly appointed CEO of Nexus Health Systems, you face simultaneous challenges across strategy, finance, operations, marketing, people, technology, and governance. Your decisions will determine whether Nexus survives and thrives or fails.

**Time to Complete**: 5-6 hours
**Difficulty**: Master Level
**Integration Requirement**: ALL nine phases of the MBA curriculum

## Case Questions

### Part A: Situational Assessment (Apply Phases 1, 3)

1. **Root Cause Analysis**
   - What are the fundamental reasons for Nexus's decline?
   - How much of the problem is internal vs. external?
   - What is the single most critical issue that must be addressed?

2. **Competitive Analysis**
   - What is Nexus's sustainable competitive advantage, if any?
   - How should Nexus position itself against Summit?
   - What is the threat from new entrants (Amazon, CVS, etc.)?

### Part B: Financial Strategy (Apply Phase 2)

3. **Financial Turnaround**
   - Develop a detailed plan to return to operating profitability
   - What are the most impactful cost reduction opportunities?
   - How will you address the credit rating concerns?

4. **Capital Allocation**
   - Given limited capital, what investments should be prioritized?
   - Should Nexus sell any assets? Which ones?
   - How should the health plan be positioned (grow, maintain, divest)?

5. **Portfolio Decisions**
   - What should be done with Community Hospitals A and D?
   - What is your recommendation for the rural hospital?
   - How would you present this to the board and community?

### Part C: Operational Excellence (Apply Phase 5)

6. **Productivity Improvement**
   - Develop a plan to close the labor productivity gap
   - How will you reduce agency/travel nurse costs by 75%?
   - What operational improvements will improve efficiency?

7. **Quality Improvement**
   - What are the highest-priority quality improvement initiatives?
   - How will you address hospital-acquired infections?
   - What would it take to improve Star ratings across the system?

8. **Supply Chain**
   - How would you capture the $40-60M supply chain opportunity?
   - What physician behavior changes are needed?
   - What technology/process improvements would help?

### Part D: Marketing and Growth (Apply Phase 4)

9. **Market Position**
   - How should Nexus differentiate from Summit?
   - What services/markets offer growth potential?
   - How do you stop market share erosion?

10. **Patient Experience**
    - What is your strategy to improve patient experience?
    - How important is digital/virtual care for Nexus?
    - How do you balance quality, experience, and cost?

### Part E: People and Leadership (Apply Phase 6)

11. **Leadership Team**
    - What are your priorities for rebuilding the executive team?
    - What kind of leaders do you need for this situation?
    - How will you assess and decide on current executives?

12. **Nursing Strategy**
    - How will you solve the nursing crisis?
    - What is the total investment required?
    - How long will it take to stabilize the nursing workforce?

13. **Physician Strategy**
    - How do you make the employed physician group sustainable?
    - What is your strategy to retain independent physicians?
    - How do you prevent further departures to competitors?

14. **Culture and Change**
    - What cultural changes are needed at Nexus?
    - How do you create urgency without creating panic?
    - How will you maintain employee morale during the turnaround?

### Part F: Technology and Innovation (Apply Phase 7, 8)

15. **Technology Strategy**
    - What are the highest-priority technology investments?
    - Should Nexus consolidate to single EMR? How to fund it?
    - What analytics capabilities are most important?

16. **Digital Strategy**
    - How should Nexus respond to Amazon, CVS, and telehealth?
    - What is the role of virtual care in Nexus's future?
    - How do you balance digital investment with other priorities?

### Part G: Governance and Stakeholders (Apply Phase 9)

17. **Board Management**
    - How will you manage the different factions on the board?
    - What if the board opposes hospital closures you believe are necessary?
    - How will you communicate difficult news?

18. **Stakeholder Management**
    - How do you balance the interests of competing stakeholders?
    - What is your approach to community relations?
    - How do you handle the union during the turnaround?

### Part H: Integration and Implementation (Apply All Phases)

19. **Strategic Plan**
    - Develop an integrated 3-year strategic plan for Nexus
    - What is the vision for Nexus at the end of Year 3?
    - What are the 5 strategic priorities and why?

20. **Implementation Roadmap**
    - Create a detailed 100-day plan as incoming CEO
    - What are the key milestones for Year 1?
    - What metrics will you track to measure progress?

21. **Risk Assessment**
    - What are the biggest risks to the turnaround?
    - What could cause the plan to fail?
    - What contingency plans should be in place?

22. **Board Presentation**
    - Prepare an executive presentation for the board
    - What difficult recommendations will you make?
    - How will you gain board support for tough decisions?
