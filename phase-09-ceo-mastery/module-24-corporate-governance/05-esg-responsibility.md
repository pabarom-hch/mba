## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

1. **Define** ESG factors and their materiality to business performance
2. **Apply** major ESG reporting frameworks and standards
3. **Integrate** sustainability into corporate strategy
4. **Evaluate** ESG ratings and investor expectations
5. **Design** governance structures for ESG oversight
6. **Measure** and report ESG performance effectively

## Introduction: The ESG Imperative

Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) factors have moved from the periphery to the center of corporate strategy and investor decision-making. Companies that effectively manage ESG risks and opportunities create long-term value while addressing stakeholder expectations.

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: Materiality Assessment

**Scenario:** You are the Chief Sustainability Officer of a global apparel and footwear company. Conduct a materiality assessment:

1. Identify the top 10 ESG issues for your industry
2. Prioritize them by financial materiality and impact materiality
3. Identify which stakeholders care most about each issue
4. Recommend which issues should be strategic priorities

### Exercise 2: Climate Strategy

**Scenario:** Your company has committed to net zero by 2050 but has not yet set near-term targets or developed a transition plan.

**Develop:**
1. Scope 1, 2, and 3 emission reduction targets for 2030
2. Key decarbonization initiatives
3. Governance structure for climate oversight
4. KPIs to track progress

### Exercise 3: ESG Disclosure Review

**Scenario:** Review your company's current ESG disclosure against ISSB/IFRS S1 and S2 requirements:

1. Identify gaps in current disclosure
2. Prioritize disclosure improvements
3. Develop a 3-year roadmap to compliance
4. Identify data and process improvements needed
