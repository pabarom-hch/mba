### Instrumental vs. Normative Stakeholder Theory

| Perspective | View | Implication |
|-------------|------|-------------|
| **Instrumental** | Stakeholder management as means to profit | Manage stakeholders strategically for competitive advantage |
| **Normative** | Stakeholders have intrinsic worth | Ethical obligation to consider all stakeholder interests |
| **Descriptive** | Organizations do have stakeholders | Understand how managers actually consider stakeholders |

### Stakeholder Classification

| Type | Attributes | Example | Management Approach |
|------|-----------|---------|---------------------|
| **Dormant** | Power only | Fired employees with lawsuit potential | Monitor |
| **Discretionary** | Legitimacy only | Community groups | Engage voluntarily |
| **Demanding** | Urgency only | Single-issue protesters | Monitor |
| **Dominant** | Power + Legitimacy | Major institutional investors | High engagement |
| **Dangerous** | Power + Urgency | Hostile acquirers | Defensive response |
| **Dependent** | Legitimacy + Urgency | Disaster victims | Advocacy needed |
| **Definitive** | All three | Major shareholders, regulators | Priority attention |

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: Stakeholder Mapping

**Scenario:** A pharmaceutical company is considering acquiring a smaller biotech firm. Map the stakeholders using both the Salience model and Power/Interest matrix.

**Stakeholders to classify:**
- Current shareholders of both companies
- Employees of target company
- FDA (regulator)
- Patient advocacy groups
- Current customers/healthcare providers
- Competitors
- Local community where target is headquartered

### Exercise 2: Engagement Strategy Design

**Scenario:** Your company plans to build a new manufacturing facility in a community that has previously opposed industrial development.

Design an engagement strategy covering:
1. Stakeholder identification
2. Key concerns to address
3. Engagement methods and timeline
4. Success metrics
5. Escalation process if opposition grows

### Exercise 3: Trade-off Resolution

**Scenario:** A retail company faces pressure from investors to reduce costs by 10%. Options include:
- Reduce employee hours and benefits
- Close underperforming stores
- Switch to lower-cost suppliers (possibly lower quality)
- Increase prices

Analyze the stakeholder implications of each option and recommend an approach that balances interests.
