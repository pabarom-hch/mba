## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

1. **Describe** the roles and responsibilities of the board of directors
2. **Differentiate** between board structures and compositions
3. **Analyze** the functions of key board committees
4. **Evaluate** director duties and fiduciary obligations
5. **Assess** board effectiveness and performance
6. **Apply** best practices for board recruitment and succession

## Introduction: The Board's Critical Role

The board of directors serves as the bridge between shareholders and management, providing strategic oversight while ensuring accountability. An effective board protects shareholder interests, guides strategy, and ensures the company operates ethically and legally.

### Board Size and Composition Best Practices

| Factor | Recommendation | Rationale |
|--------|---------------|-----------|
| **Size** | 8-12 members | Balance deliberation and diversity |
| **Independence** | Majority independent | Objective oversight |
| **CEO/Chair** | Separate or lead director | Balance of power |
| **Executive directors** | 1-2 maximum | Prevent management capture |
| **Term limits** | 9-15 years typical | Fresh perspectives |
| **Age limits** | 72-75 common | Energy and relevance |
| **Meeting frequency** | 6-10 per year minimum | Adequate oversight |

- At least one "financial expert"
- Direct access to internal and external auditors
- Authority to hire independent advisors
- Pre-approve audit and non-audit services
