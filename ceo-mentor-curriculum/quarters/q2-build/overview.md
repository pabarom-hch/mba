Carnegie brings the practical experience of building one of history's greatest industrial enterprises. His contribution is **how to build organizations**:

**Reading:** Think and Grow Rich (Weeks 20-26)

## Core Concepts

### The Mastermind Principle
Carnegie's greatest insight: "No two minds ever come together without thereby creating a third, invisible, intangible force." When minds align toward a common purpose, the combined intelligence exceeds the sum of parts.

### Definiteness of Purpose
Hill's foundational principle: You must know exactly what you want. Vague desires produce vague results. A definite major purpose, held constantly in mind, directs all energy toward achievement.

### Building Through Others
Carnegie said: "Here lies a man who knew how to enlist in his service better men than himself." The builder's job is not to be the best at everything but to assemble and align the best people.

### Persistence
Hill found this common to all successful people: temporary defeat is not permanent failure. The difference between those who succeed and those who don't is often simply continuing after setback.

### Wealth as Responsibility
Carnegie's Gospel of Wealth: The wealthy are trustees of their fortune, responsible for distributing it for public good. Wealth is not an end but a tool.

## The Carnegie-Hill Connection

Carnegie commissioned Hill in 1908 to study success systematically. Hill interviewed over 500 successful Americans over 20 years, including Carnegie, Ford, Edison, and others. Think and Grow Rich was the culmination.

Carnegie provided the hypothesis; Hill tested it against 500 cases.

This quarter, you benefit from both: Carnegie's lived experience building an empire, and Hill's codified principles validated across hundreds of successful lives.
