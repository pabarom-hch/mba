# Week 13: Compounding — The Eighth Wonder of the World

**Quarter:** Q1 — THINK
**Mentor:** Warren Buffett
**Theme:** The power of small advantages sustained over time

> "My wealth has come from a combination of living in America, some lucky genes, and compound interest." — Warren Buffett

---

## Overview

This final week of Q1 addresses the most powerful force in finance and life: compounding. Einstein allegedly called compound interest "the eighth wonder of the world." Whether he said it or not, it's true.

Compounding applies not just to money but to knowledge, skills, relationships, and reputation. Small advantages, maintained consistently over time, become insurmountable.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Compounding is the most powerful force.** Small advantages sustained over time become insurmountable.

2. **Everything compounds—not just money.** Knowledge, relationships, reputation, skills.

3. **Consistency is the key.** Interruptions reset the counter. Continuity allows accumulation.

4. **Time is the variable.** Compounding requires patience. Short-term thinking prevents it.

5. **Design for compounding.** Build businesses, relationships, and habits that accumulate value over time.

## The Compounding Mindset

Ask of every activity:
- Does this compound, or is it a one-time transaction?
- Am I building something that will be more valuable tomorrow?
- What am I investing in that will pay dividends for decades?
