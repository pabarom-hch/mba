## The Bias Taxonomy

### Category 1: How We Relate to Others
**Biases:** Liking, Disliking, Reciprocation, Association, Social Proof, Fairness, Envy, Authority

**Theme:** Our judgment is deeply affected by social dynamics.

**Protection:** Evaluate ideas separately from sources. Seek diverse perspectives. Build mechanisms for independent judgment.

### Category 2: How We See Ourselves
**Biases:** Self-Regard, Denial, Over-Optimism

**Theme:** We see ourselves more favorably than warranted.

**Protection:** Seek external calibration. Apply adjustment factors. Ask what you're not seeing.

### Category 3: How We Make Commitments
**Biases:** Doubt-Avoidance, Inconsistency-Avoidance, Deprival Super-Reaction

**Theme:** We rush to commit and resist updating.

**Protection:** Slow down decisions. Pre-commit to reconsideration triggers. Ignore sunk costs.

### Category 4: How We Process Information
**Biases:** Availability, Contrast, Reason-Respecting, Stress

**Theme:** Context and state distort how we evaluate information.

**Protection:** Seek base rates. Evaluate absolutely, not just relatively. Build in cooling-off periods. Require genuine reasons.

### Category 5: How They Combine
**Bias:** Lollapalooza Tendency

**Theme:** Multiple biases create exponentially powerful effects.

**Protection:** Map bias constellations. Introduce countervailing forces. Be suspicious when everything aligns.

## The CEO's Bias Defense System

### Personal Practices

**Daily:**
- Notice when bias might be operating
- Pause before reacting to emotional triggers
- Ask "What am I not seeing?"

**Weekly:**
- Review major decisions for bias influence
- Seek disconfirming information
- Check stress levels and adjust accordingly

**Monthly:**
- External calibration (feedback, data, outside views)
- Audit ongoing commitments for sunk cost traps
- Review prediction accuracy to calibrate optimism

### Team Practices

**In meetings:**
- Collect written opinions before discussion
- Assign devil's advocates
- Separate evaluation from personal relationships
- Create safety for dissent

**In decisions:**
- Use pre-committed decision criteria
- Build in delays for major choices
- Require genuine reasoning
- Track decisions and outcomes

**In culture:**
- Reward bad news delivery
- Model admitting mistakes
- Celebrate course corrections
- Build bias literacy

### Organizational Systems

**For major decisions:**
- Decision documentation requirements
- Mandatory input from diverse perspectives
- External review for highest-stakes choices
- Post-decision tracking and learning

**For ongoing operations:**
- Metrics that counteract availability bias
- Base rate information readily accessible
- Regular fresh-start evaluations
- Kill criteria for investments

## The Three-Week Synthesis

### The Core Insight

Human thinking is systematically biased. These aren't occasional errors—they're default settings. Understanding them is necessary but not sufficient.

### The Practical Framework

**Know:** The 25 biases and how they operate
**Recognize:** When biases are affecting you or your organization
**Design:** Systems that compensate for predictable errors
**Cultivate:** Culture where bias-checking is normal

### The Key Distinctions

**Awareness vs. Structure:**
Awareness helps modestly. Structure helps consistently. Build systems, not just understanding.

**Individual vs. Combination:**
Single biases nudge. Combined biases force. Watch for lollapalooza patterns.

**Knowing vs. Overcoming:**
You can't eliminate biases. You can design around them. Accept limitation; compensate systematically.
