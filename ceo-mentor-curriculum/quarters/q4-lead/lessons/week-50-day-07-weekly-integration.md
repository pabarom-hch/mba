# Week 50, Day 7: Weekly Integration — Developing Other Leaders

> "The greatest legacy a leader can leave is having developed other leaders who will continue to develop leaders." — John Maxwell

## The Core Insight

This week has been about transformation—from addition leadership to multiplication leadership, from personal contribution to legacy creation. The concepts build on each other: understanding multiplication mathematics, identifying potential leaders, following a systematic development process, investing the necessary time, reproducing yourself through others, and building culture that makes development self-sustaining. Together, they form a comprehensive approach to leadership multiplication.

Today we integrate these elements into a cohesive framework for developing other leaders.

## Integrated Framework: The MULTIPLY Method

### M — Model Excellence

Before you can develop leaders, you must be worth reproducing:
- Lead yourself effectively
- Demonstrate the values you want to reproduce
- Continue your own development visibly
- Show both strength and authentic vulnerability

### U — Uncover Potential

Use the POTENTIAL framework to identify who to develop:
- **P**roven track record
- **O**pportunity seeker
- **T**hinking ability
- **E**motional intelligence
- **N**atural influence
- **T**eachability
- **I**ntegrity
- **A**lignment
- **L**eadership of self

### L — Lead Through Development

Follow the 5-step method:
1. I do, you watch (with narration)
2. I do, you help (participation)
3. You do, I help (supported leadership)
4. You do, I watch (independent leadership)
5. You do, someone else watches (multiplication)

### T — Time Investment

Protect and prioritize development time:
- Block development time in your calendar
- Defend blocks against competing demands
- Use time efficiently through group learning and delegation
- Accept short-term cost for long-term liberation

### I — Impart Your Essence

Reproduce principles while allowing unique expression:
- Transfer core values and beliefs
- Teach decision-making frameworks
- Share leadership practices
- Allow personal style to emerge
- Release them to lead their way

### P — Produce Culture

Build systems that make development self-sustaining:
- Visible leadership commitment
- Structures and systems
- Rituals and rhythms
- Language and stories
- Accountability and consequences

### L — Legacy Orientation

Keep the end in mind:
- Measure success by multiplication generations
- Build for sustainability beyond your tenure
- Celebrate developed leaders' success
- Work toward your own obsolescence

### Y — Yield to Results

Trust the process and evaluate outcomes:
- Track development progress systematically
- Adjust approach based on results
- Celebrate multiplication success
- Learn from development failures

## Case Study: The Complete Transformation

### The Starting Point

When Michael Torres became CEO of RegionalBank, he was a classic addition leader. He worked 70-hour weeks. Every significant decision came through him. He had no leadership pipeline—when leaders left, he scrambled to fill positions. His board worried about succession.

### The Transformation Journey

**Year 1: Personal Transformation**

Michael attended a leadership conference where he encountered the multiplication concept. He committed to transformation despite skepticism.

Actions:
- Assessed his top 20 people using POTENTIAL framework
- Identified 6 with genuine leadership potential
- Began weekly one-on-ones focused on development
- Started narrating his decision-making to developing leaders

Results:
- Time pressure increased initially (development time on top of existing work)
- Two promising leaders emerged as clearly high-potential
- Michael's own leadership clarity improved through teaching

**Year 2: Development Momentum**

Michael deepened development investment despite pressure.

Actions:
- Moved two leaders to "You do, I help" stage
- Created a leadership curriculum taught monthly
- Began delegating significant decisions to developing leaders
- Tracked development progress systematically

Results:
- First time liberation as developed leaders handled decisions
- One developed leader ready for promotion
- Development culture began forming in Michael's direct team

**Year 3: Multiplication Begins**

The investment started compounding.

Actions:
- Promoted first developed leader to senior role
- Required her to begin developing her own leaders
- Expanded development investment to second tier of potential
- Began culture work—requiring development in performance reviews

Results:
- Two developed leaders operating independently
- Second-generation development beginning
- Michael's weekly hours dropped below 60 for first time in years

**Year 4-5: Culture Transformation**

Michael focused on sustainability.

Actions:
- Made development a promotion criterion throughout organization
- Created "Leadership Tree" recognition system
- Documented development methodology for organization use
- Began succession planning for his own role

Results:
- Leadership culture established—development was "how we do things"
- Third-generation leaders emerging
- Multiple succession candidates ready for CEO role
- Michael transitioned to Chairman with organization thriving

### The Legacy

Ten years after Michael's transformation began, RegionalBank was known for developing leaders. Competitors recruited from RegionalBank for their leadership talent. The development culture survived multiple leadership transitions. Michael's multiplication legacy extended far beyond his direct develops.

## Reflection Questions for Integration

1. **Commitment Check**: Are you genuinely committed to the multi-year journey of becoming a multiplication leader? What would derail this commitment? How will you stay committed when results are slow?

2. **Identity Shift**: How attached are you to being the hero—the problem solver, the smartest person, the one everyone needs? What would it take to find equal satisfaction in developing heroes?

3. **First Steps**: Of all the concepts this week, which one requires your immediate attention? What will you do tomorrow to begin that work?

4. **Accountability Structure**: How will you hold yourself accountable for development investment? Who will ask you hard questions about your progress?

5. **Legacy Vision**: Imagine your leadership legacy five years from now. What would it look like if you fully embraced multiplication? What would it look like if you continued with addition? Which vision do you want?
