## The Complete Personal Development Framework

### The Foundation: Self as Primary Asset

**Key realizations:**
- You are your most valuable asset
- Income rarely exceeds personal development
- Work on self first, then work on work
- The self is the leverage point

### The Ceiling: Limits Are Internal

**The truth:**
- Your ceiling is your current development
- For things to change, you must change
- For things to get better, you must get better
- The limit is you; the solution is also you

**The approach:**
- Identify ceilings
- Understand their nature
- Plan to raise them
- Execute the development

### The Priority: Learning Is Non-Optional

**The commitment:**
- Learning as priority, not interest
- Self-education is ongoing
- Formal education ends; self-education never does
- Learning is scheduled and protected

### The Method: Reading as Foundation

**The practice:**
- 30 minutes minimum daily
- 52+ books per year
- Active reading with notes
- Application of what's learned

### The Acceleration: Mentors and Models

**The strategy:**
- Direct mentors for guidance
- Distant mentors for study
- Dead mentors for wisdom
- Peer mentors for support

### The Commitment: Investment as Proof

**The investment:**
- 5-10% of income minimum
- Books, courses, coaching, experiences
- Time, energy, attention
- Proportionate to aspirations

## The Rohn Legacy for Personal Development

### His Core Teaching

**Work harder on yourself:**
- Before working on business
- Continuously and deliberately
- With serious investment
- As the path to everything else

### His Practice

**What Rohn modeled:**
- Daily reading (hours, not minutes)
- Constant learning
- Mentor relationships
- Teaching to deepen learning
- Investment in self

### His Challenge

**What he would ask:**
- How much time on self vs. job?
- What are you learning now?
- Who are your mentors?
- How much are you investing?
- Are you working harder on yourself?

## Key Quote for the Week

> "Work harder on yourself than you do on your job."

This is Rohn's signature principle. Most people work hard at their job and neglect themselves. The result is a person who can't rise beyond their current development. Work harder on yourself. Every improvement in you improves your work. Every development in you develops your results. The job has limits; you have none. Work on the unlimited. Work harder on yourself.
