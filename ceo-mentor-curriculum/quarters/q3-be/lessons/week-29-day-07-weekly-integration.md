## The Complete Daily Discipline Framework

### The Foundation: Daily is the Unit

**Key realizations:**
- Success is daily, not a single event
- What you do every day determines results
- Consistency trumps intensity
- Daily is where compound happens

### The Trade-Off: Discipline vs. Regret

**The choice:**
- Pain of discipline (ounces)
- Pain of regret (tons)
- One is temporary, one is permanent
- Choose wisely

**The practice:**
- Choose discipline over comfort
- Accept temporary discomfort
- Prevent permanent regret
- Make the trade consciously

### The Nature: Simple, Not Easy

**The paradox:**
- Disciplines that work are simple
- Simple is easy to do
- Simple is also easy not to do
- Same simplicity, opposite outcomes

**The practice:**
- Don't dismiss simple
- Don't seek complex
- Do the basics
- Do them daily

### The Power: Compound Effect

**The mechanism:**
- Small + Consistent + Time = Massive
- Invisible phase before visible results
- Patience through invisibility
- Trust the math

**The practice:**
- Calculate compound over years
- Commit through invisible phase
- Never reset the compound
- Let time work

### The Protection: Non-Negotiables

**The requirement:**
- True non-negotiables, not wishful ones
- Protected from self and others
- Executed regardless
- No exceptions

**The practice:**
- Select 3-5 key disciplines
- Commit absolutely
- Protect completely
- Execute automatically

### The Sustainability: Habit Formation

**The solution:**
- Habit replaces willpower
- Cue → Routine → Reward
- Automaticity is the goal
- Habit keeps you going

**The practice:**
- Design complete habit loops
- Start smaller than you think
- Stack on existing habits
- Reduce friction

## The Rohn Legacy for Daily Disciplines

### His Core Teaching

**Daily is everything:**
- Success is daily
- Failure is daily
- Results compound from daily
- Life is shaped daily

### His Essential Disciplines

**Rohn's recommended dailies:**
1. Read (at least 30 minutes)
2. Keep a journal
3. Set and review goals
4. Exercise
5. Work on yourself
6. Protect your time

### His Challenge

**What he would ask:**
- What do you do every day?
- Are these the right disciplines?
- Are they truly non-negotiable?
- Are they becoming habits?
- What's the 10-year compound?
