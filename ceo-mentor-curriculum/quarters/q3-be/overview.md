Together, they address both the practical and ethical dimensions of self-mastery.

## The Two Mentors

### Jim Rohn: The Practical Philosopher

Rohn brings accessible, actionable wisdom for daily improvement. His contribution is **how to develop yourself practically**:
- Personal philosophy determines outcomes
- Daily disciplines create success
- You are the average of your associations
- Work harder on yourself than on your job

**Reading:** 7 Strategies for Wealth & Happiness (Weeks 27-32)

### Mahatma Gandhi: The Moral Exemplar

Gandhi brings the ethical dimension of leadership. His contribution is **how to lead through character**:
- Be the change you wish to see
- Truth as the highest principle
- Non-violence as strength
- Servant leadership and simplicity

**Reading:** The Story of My Experiments with Truth (Weeks 33-39)

## Core Concepts

### Personal Philosophy
Rohn taught that your philosophy—how you think about life—determines your actions, which determine your results. Change your philosophy, change your results.

### Daily Disciplines
Success is not a single event but the compound effect of daily disciplines. What you do daily matters more than what you do occasionally.

### Be the Change
Gandhi demonstrated that the leader must embody what they ask of others. You cannot inspire what you do not model.

### Truth (Satya)
Gandhi built his life on truth-seeking and truth-telling. Alignment with truth creates unstoppable moral force.

### Self-Mastery
Both Rohn and Gandhi emphasized that leading others requires first mastering yourself—your habits, your desires, your character.

## Application Framework

Each week's principles apply to personal development:

**Inner Work**
- What is my philosophy about this?
- What daily discipline would change my results?
- Am I modeling what I ask of others?

**Character Development**
- Where is truth being compromised?
- How can I serve rather than be served?
- What unnecessary complexity can I eliminate?

**Association Management**
- Who am I spending time with?
- Are my associations pulling me up or holding me back?
- How can I contribute to others' growth?

## The Rohn-Gandhi Connection

On the surface, Rohn and Gandhi seem different—one a business speaker, one a political leader. But they share deep convictions:

- **Personal responsibility:** Your outcomes are your responsibility
- **Character matters:** Who you are determines what you can do
- **Daily practice:** Change happens through consistent small actions
- **Service to others:** Success is measured by impact on others
- **Simplicity:** Complexity obscures; simplicity reveals

Both understood that the inner life shapes the outer world.
