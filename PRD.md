# Product Requirements Document: Executive MBA Curriculum

## Document Information
- **Version:** 1.0
- **Created:** January 2025
- **Purpose:** Self-directed executive education curriculum for CEO transition
- **Format:** Markdown files organized by phase, module, and topic

---

## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 Vision
Create a comprehensive, self-paced executive education curriculum that integrates:
- Elite MBA program content (Harvard, Stanford, Wharton caliber)
- Economics (Micro and Macro)
- Certified Financial Planning concepts
- Management consulting frameworks (McKinsey, Bain, BCG)

### 1.2 Target Learner
- Professional transitioning to CEO role
- Needs practical, applicable knowledge
- Values structured learning with clear progression
- Wants to develop strategic thinking capabilities

### 1.3 Learning Outcomes
Upon completion, the learner will be able to:
1. Read and analyze any financial statement with confidence
2. Make data-driven investment and capital allocation decisions
3. Develop and evaluate competitive strategy
4. Lead organizations through change and growth
5. Think and communicate like a top-tier management consultant
6. Navigate board relationships and corporate governance
7. Manage personal wealth effectively (equity compensation, tax planning)

### 2.2 Phase Breakdown

#### Phase 0: Consulting Mindset (Foundation)
**Purpose:** Establish the analytical framework used throughout the curriculum

- Module 0: The Consultant's Toolkit
  - Structured problem solving
  - Hypothesis-driven thinking
  - MECE principle
  - Issue trees and logic trees
  - The Pyramid Principle (communication)
  - 80/20 analysis
  - Synthesis vs. summary
  - Executive communication

#### Phase 1: Economic Foundations
**Purpose:** Understand how markets, incentives, and economies function

- Module 1: Microeconomics
- Module 2: Macroeconomics

#### Phase 2: Financial Accounting & Reporting
**Purpose:** Master the language of business

- Module 3: Financial Accounting

#### Phase 3: Corporate Finance & Valuation
**Purpose:** Make and evaluate investment decisions

- Module 4: Time Value of Money & Capital Budgeting
- Module 5: Cost of Capital & Capital Structure
- Module 6: Valuation
- Module 7: Mergers, Acquisitions & Corporate Restructuring

#### Phase 4: Financial Planning & Risk Management
**Purpose:** Personal and corporate financial decision-making

- Module 8: Investment Planning & Portfolio Theory
- Module 9: Tax Planning
- Module 10: Risk Management & Insurance
- Module 11: Retirement & Estate Planning

#### Phase 5: Strategic Management
**Purpose:** Set direction and build competitive advantage

- Module 12: Competitive Strategy
- Module 13: Corporate Strategy & Growth
- Module 14: Business Model Design

#### Phase 6: Marketing & Customers
**Purpose:** Acquire and retain customers profitably

- Module 15: Marketing Strategy

#### Phase 7: Operations & Analytics
**Purpose:** Execute and measure effectively

- Module 16: Operations Management
- Module 17: Data-Driven Decision Making

#### Phase 8: Leadership & Organization
**Purpose:** Scale through people

- Module 18: Organizational Behavior
- Module 19: Leadership
- Module 20: Negotiations
- Module 21: Managing Change

#### Phase 9: CEO Mastery
**Purpose:** Executive-specific knowledge

- Module 22: Corporate Governance
- Module 23: Ethics & Corporate Responsibility
- Module 24: CEO Communication & Presence

#### Phase 11: Social Capital & Elite Networks
**Purpose:** Navigate elite social and business environments

- Module 30: Foundations of Elite Etiquette
  - Sociology of wealth and class
  - Cultural codes and signals
  - Understatement and discretion
  - Introductions and forms of address
  - Building reputation

- Module 31: Business Etiquette Mastery
  - Formal dining protocol
  - Wine service and toasting
  - International business customs
  - Written correspondence
  - Private club conduct

- Module 32: Social Settings & Lifestyle
  - Dress codes decoded
  - Charity galas and fundraising
  - Art world navigation
  - Estate visits and weekend invitations
  - Social media and privacy

- Module 33: Building Authentic Relationships
  - From networking to relationship building
  - Genuine curiosity and reciprocity
  - Old money relationship dynamics
  - Maintaining relationships over decades
  - Legacy and reputation

## 5. Consulting Mindset Integration

### 5.1 Core Consulting Frameworks to Embed

**Problem Solving:**
- Issue trees and logic trees
- MECE (Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive)
- Hypothesis-driven approach
- Root cause analysis (5 Whys, Fishbone)

**Analysis:**
- 80/20 (Pareto) analysis
- Benchmarking
- Trend analysis
- Scenario planning

**Communication:**
- Pyramid Principle (Minto)
- SCQA (Situation, Complication, Question, Answer)
- Storylining
- Executive summary writing

**Business Frameworks:**
- Profitability framework
- Market entry framework
- M&A framework
- Pricing framework
- Operations improvement framework
- Growth strategy framework

### 5.2 Integration Approach

Every module includes:
1. **"Consulting Application" section** - How consultants use these concepts
2. **Case studies** - Structured like consulting case interviews
3. **Framework connections** - Explicit links to consulting frameworks
