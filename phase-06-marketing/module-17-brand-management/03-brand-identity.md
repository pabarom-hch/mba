# Brand Identity

## Learning Objectives

- Design comprehensive brand identity systems
- Develop brand personality and voice
- Create memorable visual identities
- Ensure consistency across touchpoints
- Build distinctive brand assets

• Brand vocabulary

VISUAL IDENTITY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SENSORY IDENTITY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

EXPERIENTIAL IDENTITY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Customer experience

## Visual Identity

### Logo Design

```
LOGO PRINCIPLES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SIMPLICITY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Simple logos are:
• Easier to recognize
• More memorable
• More versatile
• More timeless

Examples: Apple, Nike, McDonald's

DISTINCTIVENESS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Stand out from competitors
• Unique shapes
• Ownable elements
• Immediate recognition

RELEVANCE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Appropriate to category and positioning
• Not too trendy
• Reflects brand values
• Appeals to target

VERSATILITY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Works across applications:
• Large and small sizes
• Color and black/white
• Digital and print
• Horizontal and stacked

LOGO TYPES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Wordmark:      Google, Coca-Cola
Lettermark:    IBM, HBO
Symbol:        Apple, Nike
Combination:   Adidas, Mastercard
Emblem:        Starbucks, Harley-Davidson
```

## Brand Voice

### Tone and Personality

```
BRAND VOICE DIMENSIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FORMAL ←→ CASUAL
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Formal:   Professional, authoritative, serious
Casual:   Friendly, conversational, approachable

SERIOUS ←→ HUMOROUS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Serious:   Straightforward, factual, earnest
Humorous:  Witty, playful, entertaining

RESPECTFUL ←→ IRREVERENT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Respectful: Polite, traditional, deferential
Irreverent: Bold, challenging, unconventional

MATTER-OF-FACT ←→ ENTHUSIASTIC
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Matter-of-fact: Calm, understated, objective
Enthusiastic: Energetic, passionate, excited

VOICE DOCUMENTATION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For each dimension:
• Where we are on spectrum
• Why this fits our brand
• Examples of correct usage
• Examples of incorrect usage
```

### Voice Guidelines

```
VOICE GUIDELINES TEMPLATE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

WE ARE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
[Characteristic 1]: Confident but not arrogant
[Characteristic 2]: Friendly but not familiar
[Characteristic 3]: Expert but not condescending

WE SAY:                  WE DON'T SAY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"We'd love to help"      "Please contact us"
"Here's what we know"    "According to our data"
"Try this"               "You should"

VOCABULARY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Words we use:            Words we avoid:
• Discover               • Leverage
• Together               • Utilize
• Simple                 • Synergy
```

## Distinctive Brand Assets

### Building Mental Availability

```
DISTINCTIVE BRAND ASSETS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Elements that trigger brand recall without the
brand name being present

ASSET TYPES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Logos:          Nike swoosh, Apple apple
Colors:         Tiffany blue, McDonald's yellow
Taglines:       "Just Do It", "I'm Lovin' It"
Characters:     Gecko (GEICO), Ronald McDonald
Sounds:         Intel chime, Netflix "ta-dum"
Shapes:         Coca-Cola bottle, Toblerone
Celebrities:    Flo (Progressive), Mayhem (Allstate)

ASSET STRENGTH CRITERIA:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Fame:         % who recognize the asset
Uniqueness:   % who link to correct brand

Best assets are both famous AND unique

BUILDING DISTINCTIVE ASSETS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Consistency: Use same assets repeatedly
• Prominence: Feature prominently in communications
• Linking: Always connect to brand name initially
• Protection: Legal and consistent enforcement
```

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Identity is comprehensive** - Verbal, visual, sensory, experiential
2. **Consistency builds recognition** - Same elements, everywhere
3. **Simplicity aids memory** - Simple logos and colors work best
4. **Voice reflects personality** - How you say it matters
5. **Distinctive assets create shortcuts** - Triggers without brand name
6. **Document everything** - Brand guidelines ensure consistency
