## What Is a Brand?

### Definitions

```
BRAND DEFINITIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

LEGAL DEFINITION:
"A name, term, sign, symbol, or design, or combination
of them, intended to identify goods or services of
one seller and differentiate from competitors."
                                        — AMA

STRATEGIC DEFINITION:
"A brand is a customer's gut feeling about a product,
service, or company. It's not what you say it is,
it's what they say it is."
                                        — Marty Neumeier

ECONOMIC DEFINITION:
"A brand is a promise of quality, consistency, and
satisfaction that reduces consumer risk and enables
premium pricing."

THE COMPLETE VIEW:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Brand = Promise + Experience + Perception

Promise:      What you commit to deliver
Experience:   What customers actually get
Perception:   What lives in their minds
```

### Brand vs. Product

```
PRODUCT VS. BRAND:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PRODUCT:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Something made in factory
• Features and attributes
• Functional benefits
• Can be copied
• Exists in the physical world

BRAND:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Something made in consumer's mind
• Meaning and associations
• Emotional and symbolic benefits
• Cannot be copied
• Exists in perceptions

EXAMPLE - WATER:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Product: H2O, clear liquid, hydrates

Evian brand:       French Alps, purity, premium
Dasani brand:      Convenience, Coca-Cola, everyday
Fiji brand:        Exotic, untouched, status
SmartWater brand:  Science, enhancement, celebrity

Same product, different brands, different value
```

## Brand Equity

### Understanding Brand Equity

```
BRAND EQUITY DEFINED:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"The differential effect that knowing the brand name
has on customer response to the product or its
marketing."
                                        — Keller

AAKER'S BRAND EQUITY MODEL:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

                    BRAND EQUITY
                         │
    ┌────────┬──────────┼──────────┬────────┐
    │        │          │          │        │
    ▼        ▼          ▼          ▼        ▼
  Brand    Brand    Perceived   Brand     Other
Awareness Assoc.    Quality   Loyalty   Assets

BRAND AWARENESS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Recognition: Seen before?
• Recall: Comes to mind?
• Top-of-mind: First thought?
• Dominance: Only one?

BRAND ASSOCIATIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Attributes (product and non-product)
• Benefits (functional, experiential, symbolic)
• Attitudes (overall evaluations)
• Personality traits

PERCEIVED QUALITY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Overall quality judgment
• Relative to alternatives
• Basis for premium pricing
• Key purchase driver

BRAND LOYALTY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Behavioral (repeat purchase)
• Attitudinal (preference, advocacy)
• Attachment (emotional connection)
• Community (belonging)

OTHER ASSETS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Trademarks
• Patents
• Channel relationships
```

### Measuring Brand Equity

```
BRAND EQUITY METRICS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

AWARENESS METRICS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Unaided awareness:    % who mention unprompted
Aided awareness:      % who recognize when shown
Top-of-mind:          % who mention first
Brand salience:       Mental availability

ASSOCIATION METRICS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Attribute ratings:    Performance on key attributes
Differentiation:      Perceived uniqueness
Relevance:            Personal importance
Esteem:               Regard and respect

BEHAVIORAL METRICS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Market share:         Actual purchase behavior
Price premium:        Price vs. competitor
Loyalty rate:         Repeat purchase %
Share of wallet:      % of category spend

ATTITUDINAL METRICS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Net Promoter Score:   Promoters - Detractors
Purchase intent:      Likelihood to buy
Preference:           First choice %
Emotional connection: Brand attachment scale

FINANCIAL METRICS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Brand value:          Financial valuation
Brand contribution:   % of price from brand
Revenue premium:      Revenue vs. generic
```

## Brand Value Creation

### How Brands Create Value

```
VALUE FOR CUSTOMERS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

RISK REDUCTION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Known brands reduce perceived risk:
• Functional risk (will it work?)
• Financial risk (is it worth it?)
• Social risk (what will others think?)
• Psychological risk (will I be satisfied?)

DECISION SIMPLIFICATION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Brands serve as mental shortcuts:
• Category entry points
• Habitual purchase triggers
• Information economy

IDENTITY EXPRESSION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Brands help express who we are:
• Self-image congruence
• Status signaling
• Group belonging
• Values expression

EMOTIONAL BENEFITS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Brands create emotional responses:
• Nostalgia
• Comfort
• Excitement
• Connection
```

### Value for Companies

```
VALUE FOR ORGANIZATIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PRICE PREMIUM:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Strong brands command higher prices
Example: Apple vs. generic computer
Premium: 20-50%+ above competition

CUSTOMER LOYALTY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Loyal customers:
• Repeat purchase
• Lower price sensitivity
• Lower acquisition costs
• Word-of-mouth advocacy

TRADE LEVERAGE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Retailers want strong brands:
• Better shelf placement
• Promotional support
• Lower slotting fees
• Partnership opportunities

EXTENSION PLATFORM:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Strong brands enable:
• New product launches
• Category expansion
• Lower introduction costs
• Higher success rates

TALENT ATTRACTION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Strong brands attract:
• Better employees
• Lower recruiting costs
• Higher engagement
• Employer brand value

CRISIS RESILIENCE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Strong brands survive crises:
• Benefit of the doubt
• Faster recovery
• Maintained loyalty
• Reduced damage
```

## Building Strong Brands

### Brand Building Process

```
BRAND BUILDING STAGES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. BRAND IDENTITY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Define what the brand stands for:
• Mission and purpose
• Values and beliefs
• Personality traits
• Visual identity

2. BRAND MEANING
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Create appropriate associations:
• Performance attributes
• Imagery and symbolism
• User and usage imagery
• Category membership

3. BRAND RESPONSE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Elicit proper reactions:
• Judgments (quality, credibility)
• Feelings (warmth, excitement)
• Attitudes (positive evaluation)

4. BRAND RELATIONSHIP
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Build deep connections:
• Behavioral loyalty
• Attitudinal attachment
• Sense of community
• Active engagement
```

### Critical Success Factors

```
BRAND BUILDING ESSENTIALS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DIFFERENTIATION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Must be meaningfully different
• Unique point of difference
• Relevant to customers
• Defensible over time

CONSISTENCY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Must be consistent over time
• Same brand identity
• Coherent across touchpoints
• Long-term commitment

CREDIBILITY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Must be believable
• Deliver on promises
• Authentic to organization
• Built on real capability

MEMORABILITY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Must be easy to remember
• Distinctive brand assets
• Simple, clear identity
• Mental availability
```

## Key Takeaways

1. **Brands exist in minds** - Not factories, not logos
2. **Equity has multiple dimensions** - Awareness, associations, quality, loyalty
3. **Brands create dual value** - For customers and companies
4. **Building takes time** - Long-term, consistent investment
5. **Differentiation is essential** - Must be meaningfully different
6. **Experience must match promise** - Credibility requires delivery
