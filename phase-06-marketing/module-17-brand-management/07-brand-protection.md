## Intellectual Property Protection

### Trademark Fundamentals

```
TRADEMARK PROTECTION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

WHAT CAN BE TRADEMARKED:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Brand names (Nike)
• Logos (Apple apple)
• Slogans ("Just Do It")
• Sounds (Intel chime)
• Colors (Tiffany blue)
• Shapes (Coca-Cola bottle)
• Scents (in some jurisdictions)

TRADEMARK STRENGTH SPECTRUM:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

STRONGEST                           WEAKEST
    │                                   │
    ▼                                   ▼
Fanciful  Arbitrary  Suggestive  Descriptive  Generic
  Xerox     Apple    Netflix     General      Elevator
  Kodak     Amazon   Greyhound   Electric     Aspirin

Fanciful:   Made-up words (most protectable)
Arbitrary:  Real words, unrelated to product
Suggestive: Hints at quality, requires thought
Descriptive: Describes product (hard to protect)
Generic:    Common name (cannot protect)

REGISTRATION PROCESS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Conduct clearance search
2. File application
3. Examination by trademark office
4. Publication for opposition
5. Registration granted
6. Maintain through use and renewal
```

### IP Portfolio Management

```
BRAND IP PORTFOLIO:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CORE ELEMENTS TO PROTECT:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
□ Primary brand name
□ Sub-brand names
□ Logo variations (color, B&W)
□ Taglines and slogans
□ Domain names
□ Social media handles
□ Distinctive packaging
□ Unique sounds/jingles

PROTECTION STRATEGIES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Defensive Registration:
Register in classes you might enter
Prevents others from taking space

Geographic Coverage:
Register in all markets of interest
International systems (Madrid Protocol)

Variant Protection:
Register common misspellings
Phonetic equivalents
Translation variations

MONITORING AND ENFORCEMENT:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Watch Services:
• New trademark applications
• Domain registrations
• Social media accounts
• Marketplace listings

Enforcement Actions:
• Cease and desist letters
• Opposition proceedings
• Litigation when necessary
• Takedown requests

Balance:
Protect vigorously but don't
overreach (brand goodwill risk)
```

## Crisis Management

### Crisis Preparation

```
CRISIS PREPAREDNESS FRAMEWORK:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

VULNERABILITY ASSESSMENT:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Identify potential crisis sources:
□ Product failures/recalls
□ Executive misconduct
□ Data breaches
□ Workplace issues
□ Environmental incidents
□ Supply chain problems
□ Social media backlash
□ Activist campaigns
□ Legal/regulatory issues

Rate each: Likelihood × Impact = Priority

CRISIS TEAM:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Core Team:
• CEO (ultimate authority)
• Communications lead
• Legal counsel
• Operations lead
• HR representative

Extended Team:
• Subject matter experts
• Customer service lead
• Digital/social lead
• Investor relations

External Partners:
• PR agency
• Legal firm
• Crisis consultants

RESPONSE PROTOCOLS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
□ Escalation procedures
□ Decision authority matrix
□ Communication templates
□ Stakeholder contact lists
□ Media holding statements
□ Social media guidelines
□ Employee talking points
```

### Crisis Response

```
CRISIS RESPONSE FRAMEWORK:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PHASE 1: ASSESS (First Hours)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
□ What happened? (facts only)
□ Who is affected?
□ What is the immediate risk?
□ What do we know vs. not know?
□ Who needs to be notified?

Key Principle: Get facts before responding

PHASE 2: RESPOND (First 24 Hours)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Initial Statement Elements:
• Acknowledge the situation
• Express appropriate concern
• Commit to investigation
• Provide immediate actions
• Promise updates

DO:
• Respond quickly
• Show empathy
• Accept responsibility (if appropriate)
• Focus on affected parties
• Provide useful information

DON'T:
• Speculate or guess
• Blame others immediately
• Minimize the situation
• Go silent
• Argue with critics

PHASE 3: MANAGE (Days/Weeks)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Regular updates
• Stakeholder engagement
• Media management
• Internal communications
• Corrective actions

PHASE 4: RECOVER (Weeks/Months)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Root cause analysis
• Systemic fixes
• Reputation rebuilding
• Stakeholder reassurance
• Process improvements
```

### Crisis Communication

```
CRISIS COMMUNICATION PRINCIPLES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

THE THREE Cs:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Concern:   Show genuine empathy
Control:   Demonstrate competence
Commitment: Promise and deliver action

STAKEHOLDER PRIORITIZATION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Those directly affected (first)
2. Employees (before external)
3. Key partners/customers
4. Regulators/authorities
5. Media/public
6. Investors/analysts

MESSAGE FRAMEWORK:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Opening:
"We are aware of [situation] and take
this very seriously."

Empathy:
"Our first concern is for [affected parties]."

Facts:
"Here is what we know..."

Actions:
"We are taking the following steps..."

Commitment:
"We will keep you informed as we
learn more."

CHANNEL STRATEGY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Channel          Best For
────────────────────────────────────
Website          Detailed information, updates
Social Media     Quick updates, engagement
Press Release    Formal statements
Media Interview  Executive visibility
Email            Direct stakeholder communication
Internal Comms   Employee alignment
```

## Brand Consistency

### Global Brand Management

```
GLOBAL BRAND CONSISTENCY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

WHAT MUST BE CONSISTENT:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Core Elements:
• Brand purpose and values
• Brand positioning
• Visual identity system
• Key brand assets
• Quality standards

WHAT CAN ADAPT:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Executional Elements:
• Language and messaging
• Cultural references
• Local endorsers
• Promotional tactics
• Channel mix

ADAPTATION DECISIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                    Standardize    Adapt
                    ────────────────────────
Brand Name          Usually        Sometimes*
Logo                Yes            Minor
Tagline             Sometimes      Often
Messaging           Framework      Execution
Products            Core           Variants
Pricing             Strategy       Levels
Distribution        Model          Partners

*Adapt when: pronunciation issues, negative
meanings, cultural inappropriateness

GOVERNANCE MODEL:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Global:  Strategy, identity, guidelines
Regional: Adaptation, execution oversight
Local:   Implementation, activation
```

### Brand Guidelines

```
BRAND GUIDELINES DOCUMENT:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

BRAND FOUNDATION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Brand story and history
• Mission, vision, values
• Brand positioning
• Brand personality
• Key messages

VISUAL IDENTITY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Logo specifications
  - Primary and secondary versions
  - Clear space requirements
  - Minimum size
  - Incorrect usage examples
• Color palette
  - Primary and secondary colors
  - Color codes (CMYK, RGB, HEX, Pantone)
• Typography
  - Primary and secondary fonts
  - Hierarchy and usage
• Photography style
  - Subjects and composition
  - Treatment and mood
• Graphic elements
  - Patterns and textures
  - Icons and illustrations

VERBAL IDENTITY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Tone of voice
• Writing style guidelines
• Vocabulary (dos and don'ts)
• Tagline usage
• Boilerplate copy

APPLICATIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Business cards/stationery
• Presentations
• Digital (web, email, social)
• Advertising
• Packaging
• Signage
• Merchandise

APPROVAL PROCESS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• What requires approval
• Who approves
• How to submit
• Timeline expectations
```

## Key Takeaways

1. **Protect legally** - Register trademarks, monitor infringement
2. **Monitor constantly** - Track reputation across channels
3. **Prepare for crisis** - Plan before problems occur
4. **Respond appropriately** - Speed, empathy, action
5. **Maintain consistency** - Global standards, local execution
6. **Document everything** - Clear guidelines, easy access
