## Brand Equity Measurement

### Measurement Framework

```
BRAND EQUITY MEASUREMENT DIMENSIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

AWARENESS METRICS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Measure: How well-known is the brand?

• Unaided Awareness: % who mention without prompt
  "What brands come to mind for [category]?"

• Aided Awareness: % who recognize when shown
  "Have you heard of [brand]?"

• Top-of-Mind: % who mention first
  First brand named in unaided

• Brand Recall: Retrieve from memory with cue
  "What brand has the slogan '[tagline]'?"

ASSOCIATION METRICS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Measure: What does the brand mean?

• Attribute Ratings: Performance on key dimensions
  "How innovative is [brand]?" (1-10)

• Unique Associations: What only this brand owns
  Open-ended: "What comes to mind when you
   think of [brand]?"

• Brand Personality: Human characteristics
  "If [brand] were a person, describe them"

PERCEPTION METRICS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Measure: How is the brand evaluated?

• Perceived Quality: Overall quality judgment
  "How would you rate overall quality?"

• Value Perception: Worth vs. price
  "Is [brand] worth the price?"

• Differentiation: Distinctiveness
  "How different is [brand] from competitors?"

RELATIONSHIP METRICS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Measure: How strong is the bond?

• Loyalty: Repeat purchase likelihood
  "How likely to repurchase?"

• Attachment: Emotional connection
  "Would you miss the brand if gone?"

• Advocacy: Willingness to recommend
  Net Promoter Score (NPS)
```

## Brand Tracking

### Tracking Research Design

```
BRAND TRACKING METHODOLOGY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

TRACKING OPTIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Continuous Tracking:
Interviews every week/month
+ Detects changes quickly
+ No seasonal bias
- More expensive
- Smaller samples per period

Pulse/Wave Tracking:
Periodic studies (quarterly)
+ Larger samples
+ Cost efficient
- May miss short-term changes
- Timing effects

SAMPLE DESIGN:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Target Population:
• Category buyers
• Brand users
• Brand considerers
• General population

Sample Size:
• Per wave: 300-1,000
• Trade-off: precision vs. cost
• Subsegment analysis needs

QUESTIONNAIRE ELEMENTS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
□ Category behavior
□ Brand awareness (unaided → aided)
□ Brand usage and consideration
□ Attribute perceptions
□ Overall evaluations
□ Purchase intentions
□ Media consumption
□ Demographics
```

## Brand Valuation

### Valuation Approaches

```
BRAND VALUATION METHODS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

COST-BASED APPROACH:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What it cost to build the brand

Historical Cost:
Sum of all brand investments
(advertising, development, etc.)

Replacement Cost:
What it would cost to recreate today

Limitations:
• No connection to value created
• Ignores effectiveness
• Backward-looking

MARKET-BASED APPROACH:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What similar brands sold for

Transaction Multiples:
Brand value = Revenue × Multiple
(based on comparable deals)

Royalty Relief:
What would licensee pay?
Brand value = Revenue × Royalty Rate × Years

Limitations:
• Few comparable transactions
• Each brand is unique
• Market conditions vary

INCOME-BASED APPROACH:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Future earnings attributable to brand

Brand Earnings Method:
1. Calculate total earnings
2. Subtract tangible asset returns
3. Remaining = brand earnings
4. Discount to present value

Economic Value Added:
Brand value = Brand contribution to EVA
              ÷ Discount rate

Interbrand Method:
Brand Value = Brand Earnings × Brand Strength
            (industry multiple adjusted for
             brand strength score)
```

### Interbrand Methodology

```
INTERBRAND VALUATION MODEL:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

STEP 1: FINANCIAL ANALYSIS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Calculate branded business earnings
• Revenue from branded products
• Segment operating margin
• Remove non-operating items
• Apply tax rate
• Subtract capital charges
= Economic profit

STEP 2: ROLE OF BRAND
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What % of demand is due to brand?

Role of Brand Index (0-100%):
• Research: What drives purchase?
• Benchmark: Industry norms

Brand Earnings = Economic Profit × Role of Brand %

STEP 3: BRAND STRENGTH
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Assess 10 brand strength factors:

Internal (4 factors):
• Clarity: Clear brand values/positioning
• Commitment: Internal support for brand
• Protection: Legal protection
• Responsiveness: Ability to adapt

External (6 factors):
• Authenticity: Sound heritage
• Relevance: Meets customer needs
• Differentiation: Distinguished from others
• Consistency: Cohesive experience
• Presence: Positive presence felt
• Engagement: Customer engagement depth

Brand Strength Score → Discount Rate

STEP 4: BRAND VALUE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Brand Value = NPV of Future Brand Earnings
             (using brand-strength-based discount rate)
```

## Return on Brand Investment

### Measuring Brand ROI

```
BRAND ROI FRAMEWORK:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

BRAND INVESTMENTS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Advertising spend
• Content creation
• Sponsorships
• PR/communications
• Brand development
• Customer experience
• Brand research

BRAND RETURNS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Price Premium:
Extra revenue from higher prices
Revenue × (Brand Price - Generic Price) / Generic Price

Volume Premium:
Extra volume from brand preference
Extra Units × Margin

Loyalty Value:
Reduced churn, longer tenure
Customer Lifetime Value improvement

Acquisition Efficiency:
Lower cost to acquire customers
CAC reduction × New Customers

Extension Value:
Faster, cheaper new product launches
Launch cost savings + faster break-even

ROI CALCULATION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Brand ROI = (Brand Returns - Brand Investment)
            ───────────────────────────────────
                   Brand Investment

Challenge: Attribution
• Long-term effects
• Multiple touchpoints
• Interaction effects
```

## Key Takeaways

1. **Multi-dimensional measurement** - Awareness, perception, relationship
2. **Track consistently** - Same metrics over time
3. **Leading predicts lagging** - Awareness → Sales → Value
4. **Valuation is imperfect** - Multiple methods, triangulate
5. **Digital complements traditional** - Integrate sources
6. **Connect to business outcomes** - Brand metrics → financial results
