# Distribution Strategy

## Learning Objectives

- Understand channel functions and structures
- Design effective distribution channels
- Manage channel relationships
- Evaluate omnichannel strategies
- Address channel conflict and coordination

---

## Introduction

Place (distribution) is often the least glamorous but most strategically important element of the marketing mix. While product features can be copied and prices matched quickly, distribution networks take years to build and are difficult to replicate. This chapter covers channel design, management, and the evolution toward omnichannel strategies.

---

## Channel Fundamentals

### Why Channels Exist

```
CHANNEL FUNCTIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

TRANSACTION FUNCTIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Buying: Purchasing from producers
• Selling: Promoting to customers
• Risk-taking: Ownership transfer

LOGISTICAL FUNCTIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Assortment: Creating product variety
• Storage: Holding inventory
• Sorting: Breaking bulk into smaller lots
• Transportation: Moving goods

FACILITATING FUNCTIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Financing: Extending credit
• Grading: Quality standardization
• Information: Market research, communication

VALUE CREATION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Channels bridge gaps between producers and consumers:

• Time gap: Storage makes products available when needed
• Place gap: Transportation brings products to customers
• Possession gap: Transfer of ownership
• Information gap: Product knowledge and awareness
• Value gap: Packaging, display, service add value
```

### Channel Levels

```
CHANNEL STRUCTURES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DIRECT CHANNEL (Zero level):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Producer ─────────────────────────────► Consumer

Examples: Dell, Tesla, D2C brands
Pros: Control, margins, customer data
Cons: Investment, reach, scale

ONE-LEVEL CHANNEL:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Producer ──────► Retailer ──────────► Consumer

Examples: Most consumer goods
Pros: Reach, retailer expertise
Cons: Margin sharing, less control

TWO-LEVEL CHANNEL:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Producer ──► Wholesaler ──► Retailer ──► Consumer

Examples: Grocery, convenience products
Pros: Efficiency, broad distribution
Cons: Multiple margins, distant from consumer

THREE-LEVEL CHANNEL:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Producer ─► Agent ─► Wholesaler ─► Retailer ─► Consumer

Examples: International distribution
Pros: Access to distant markets
Cons: Low control, many intermediaries

B2B CHANNELS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Producer ───────────────────────────► Business buyer
Producer ──► Distributor ──────────► Business buyer
Producer ──► Agent ──► Distributor ─► Business buyer
```

## Channel Design

### Channel Design Process

```
CHANNEL DESIGN FRAMEWORK:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

STEP 1: ANALYZE CUSTOMER NEEDS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What do customers want from channels?

• Lot size: Bulk or individual units?
• Waiting time: Immediate or can wait?
• Spatial convenience: Near or willing to travel?
• Product variety: One-stop or specialist?
• Service backup: Self-service or assisted?

STEP 2: SET CHANNEL OBJECTIVES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Balance customer needs with constraints:

• Coverage: What % of market to reach?
• Control: How much influence over presentation?
• Cost: Channel investment vs. margins?
• Flexibility: Ability to adapt?

STEP 3: IDENTIFY CHANNEL ALTERNATIVES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For each alternative, specify:
• Types of intermediaries
• Number of intermediaries
• Terms and responsibilities

STEP 4: EVALUATE ALTERNATIVES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Criteria:
• Economic: Cost, margin, breakeven
• Control: Influence over customer experience
• Adaptive: Flexibility for future changes

STEP 5: SELECT AND IMPLEMENT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Choose channel strategy
Recruit partners
Develop agreements
Launch and monitor
```

## Channel Conflict

### Types of Conflict

```
CHANNEL CONFLICT TYPES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

VERTICAL CONFLICT:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Between levels (manufacturer vs. retailer)

Causes:
• Margin disagreements
• Territorial violations
• Policy differences
• Direct sales competition

Example: Apple selling direct vs. through retailers

HORIZONTAL CONFLICT:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Between same level (retailer vs. retailer)

Causes:
• Territorial overlap
• Price undercutting
• Promotion interference

Example: Two auto dealers in same territory

MULTICHANNEL CONFLICT:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Between different channel types

Causes:
• Online vs. offline competition
• D2C vs. retail
• Price differences across channels

Example: Amazon vs. traditional retail
```

### Conflict Resolution

```
CONFLICT MANAGEMENT STRATEGIES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PREVENTION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Clear role definition
• Protected territories
• Differentiated products by channel
• Consistent pricing policies
• Channel-specific SKUs

RESOLUTION MECHANISMS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Regular communication forums
• Joint planning processes
• Mediation procedures
• Clear escalation paths

STRUCTURAL SOLUTIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Product differentiation:
• Different SKUs for different channels
• Channel-exclusive products
• Bundle differences

Pricing consistency:
• MAP (Minimum Advertised Price) policies
• Consistent promotional timing
• Territory-based pricing

Role specialization:
• Channel does what it does best
• Compensate for referrals
• Lead distribution rules
```

## Omnichannel Strategy

### Omnichannel Framework

```
EVOLUTION OF CHANNEL STRATEGY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SINGLE CHANNEL:
One channel for all interactions
Example: Physical store only

MULTICHANNEL:
Multiple separate channels
Channels operate independently
Limited integration
Example: Store + website (separate)

CROSS-CHANNEL:
Some channel integration
Customer can start in one, finish in another
Example: Research online, buy in store

OMNICHANNEL:
Seamless integration across all channels
Unified customer experience
Example: Buy online, pick up in store, return anywhere

THE OMNICHANNEL CUSTOMER JOURNEY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Research        →    Mobile app
Compare         →    Website
Try             →    Store
Purchase        →    Website
Pickup          →    Store
Support         →    Chat
Return          →    Store

All touchpoints connected, consistent experience
```

### Omnichannel Capabilities

```
OMNICHANNEL REQUIREMENTS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

UNIFIED CUSTOMER VIEW:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Single customer database
• Cross-channel identity recognition
• Complete interaction history
• Preference tracking

INTEGRATED INVENTORY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Real-time visibility across locations
• Ship from store capability
• Endless aisle (order in-store for delivery)
• Reserve online, pick up in store

CONSISTENT PRICING AND PROMOTION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Same prices across channels
• Unified promotion system
• Cross-channel loyalty programs
• Consistent policies

FLEXIBLE FULFILLMENT:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Buy online, pick up in store (BOPIS)
• Buy in store, ship to home
• Buy online, return in store
• Same-day delivery options

TECHNOLOGY INTEGRATION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Unified commerce platform
• Customer data platform (CDP)
• Order management system
• Point of sale integration
```

### D2C Strategy

```
DIRECT-TO-CONSUMER (D2C):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

D2C BENEFITS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Higher margins (no intermediary)
• Direct customer relationship
• First-party data collection
• Brand control
• Faster feedback loops
• Personalization capability

D2C CHALLENGES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Customer acquisition cost
• Fulfillment complexity
• Service infrastructure
• Reach limitations
• Channel conflict

D2C SUCCESS FACTORS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Strong brand
• Differentiated product
• Digital marketing capability
• Efficient logistics
• Customer service excellence

HYBRID D2C + RETAIL:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Many brands combine:
• D2C for brand experience and data
• Retail for reach and convenience

Example: Nike
D2C (Nike.com, Nike stores): Growing to 50%
Wholesale (Foot Locker, etc.): Selective partners
Strategy: Premium distribution only
```

## Key Takeaways

1. **Channels create value** - Not just cost, but service and reach
2. **Match intensity to product** - Convenience = intensive, specialty = exclusive
3. **Economics drive structure** - Direct vs. indirect break-even
4. **Manage partners actively** - Selection, motivation, performance
5. **Conflict is natural** - Design structures to minimize, processes to resolve
6. **Omnichannel is expected** - Seamless experience across all touchpoints
