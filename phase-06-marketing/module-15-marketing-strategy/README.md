# Module 15: Marketing Strategy

## Overview

Marketing strategy determines how firms create, communicate, and deliver value to customers. This module covers the fundamental frameworks for analyzing markets, segmenting customers, positioning products, and designing integrated marketing programs. From the 4Ps to modern digital marketing, you'll learn to develop data-driven marketing strategies that drive growth.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

1. Analyze markets and identify attractive opportunities
2. Segment customers and select target markets
3. Develop compelling positioning strategies
4. Design integrated marketing mix strategies
5. Apply the 4Ps framework effectively
6. Evaluate marketing performance and ROI
7. Adapt marketing strategies for digital environments

## Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning

```
STP FRAMEWORK:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SEGMENTATION (Divide the market):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Bases for segmentation:
• Geographic: Region, density, climate
• Demographic: Age, income, education, occupation
• Psychographic: Lifestyle, values, personality
• Behavioral: Usage, loyalty, benefits sought

Good segments are:
• Measurable (can size and profile)
• Substantial (large enough to serve)
• Accessible (can reach effectively)
• Differentiable (respond differently)
• Actionable (can design programs)

TARGETING (Select segments):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Evaluate segments on:
• Segment attractiveness (size, growth, competition)
• Company fit (capabilities, strategy alignment)

Targeting strategies:
• Undifferentiated: Same offer to all
• Differentiated: Different offers to each segment
• Concentrated: Focus on one segment
• Micro-marketing: Individual customization

POSITIONING (Differentiate):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Create distinctive place in customer's mind

Positioning statement:
For [target segment]
Who [needs/wants]
[Brand] is a [category]
That [key benefit]
Because [reason to believe]

Positioning dimensions:
• Product attributes
• Benefits delivered
• Usage occasions
• User type
• Competitor comparison
• Value/quality
```

## Marketing Metrics

```
KEY MARKETING METRICS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

MARKET METRICS:
• Market share (volume and value)
• Market growth rate
• Penetration rate
• Share of voice

CUSTOMER METRICS:
• Customer acquisition cost (CAC)
• Customer lifetime value (CLV)

• Customer satisfaction (CSAT)

BRAND METRICS:
• Brand awareness (aided/unaided)
• Brand preference
• Brand equity
• Purchase intent

FINANCIAL METRICS:
• Marketing ROI
• Revenue per customer
• Gross margin
• Marketing expense ratio

DIGITAL METRICS:
• Website traffic / Conversion rate
• Cost per click (CPC) / Cost per acquisition (CPA)
• Click-through rate (CTR)
• Email open/click rates
• Social engagement
```

## Customer Lifetime Value

```
CLV CALCULATION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CLV = (Average Purchase Value × Purchase Frequency
      × Customer Lifespan) × Profit Margin

EXAMPLE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Average purchase: $100
Purchases per year: 4
Customer lifespan: 5 years
Gross margin: 40%

CLV = $100 × 4 × 5 × 0.40 = $800

STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• CAC should be < CLV (ideally CAC < CLV/3)
• High-CLV segments justify premium acquisition
• Retention investment returns compound
• CLV/CAC ratio indicates marketing efficiency
```
