# Promotion Strategy

## Learning Objectives

- Understand integrated marketing communications (IMC)
- Apply the promotion mix effectively
- Design advertising and media strategies
- Leverage digital marketing channels
- Measure marketing communication effectiveness

---

## Introduction

Promotion encompasses all the ways companies communicate with customers to inform, persuade, and remind them about products and brands. Modern promotion has evolved from one-way advertising to interactive, data-driven, multi-channel communications. This chapter covers the promotion mix and its strategic application.

---

## Integrated Marketing Communications

### IMC Framework

```
INTEGRATED MARKETING COMMUNICATIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DEFINITION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"A strategic business process used to plan, develop,
execute, and evaluate coordinated, measurable,
persuasive brand communications over time with
consumers, customers, prospects, and other targeted,
relevant audiences."
                              — Don Schultz

IMC PRINCIPLES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Consistent message across all touchpoints
• Customer-centric (not channel-centric)
• Synergy between channels
• Data-driven targeting
• Measurable outcomes

IMC PLANNING PROCESS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Identify target audience
2. Define communication objectives
3. Determine message strategy
4. Select channels and media
5. Set budget
6. Execute campaigns
7. Measure and optimize

THE MESSAGE CONSISTENCY TEST:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
All communications should answer the same way:
• What is the brand?
• What does it stand for?
• Why should I care?
• What makes it different?
```

## Advertising

### Advertising Strategy

```
ADVERTISING DECISION FRAMEWORK:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

THE 5 Ms OF ADVERTISING:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Mission:   What is the objective?
Money:     What is the budget?
Message:   What will we say?
Media:     Where will we say it?
Measurement: How will we evaluate?

ADVERTISING OBJECTIVES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Informative:    Create awareness, knowledge
                "Introducing our new product..."

Persuasive:     Create preference, conviction
                "Better than the competition..."

Reminder:       Maintain awareness, loyalty
                "Don't forget about us..."

Reinforcement:  Reassure recent buyers
                "You made the right choice..."

DAGMAR MODEL (Defining Advertising Goals for
              Measured Advertising Results):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Awareness → Comprehension → Conviction → Action

Set measurable objectives at each stage
```

### Message Strategy

```
MESSAGE DEVELOPMENT:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CREATIVE BRIEF ELEMENTS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Target audience: Who are we talking to?
• Insight: What do we know about them?
• Objective: What do we want them to do?
• Key message: Single most important point
• Reason to believe: Why should they believe us?
• Tone: How should we sound?
• Mandatories: What must be included?

MESSAGE APPEALS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Rational:
• Feature-based
• Comparative
• Price/value
• News/announcement

Emotional:
• Fear (insurance, security)
• Humor (engagement)
• Love/warmth (family brands)
• Pride (luxury, achievement)
• Joy (experiences)

Moral:
• Social responsibility
• Environmental
• Ethical consumption

CREATIVE EXECUTION STYLES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Slice of life (everyday situation)
• Lifestyle (aspirational)
• Fantasy (imagination)
• Mood/image (atmosphere)
• Musical (jingle, song)
• Personality symbol (mascot)
• Technical expertise (demonstration)
• Scientific evidence (research)
• Testimonial (endorsement)
```

### Media Strategy

```
MEDIA PLANNING FRAMEWORK:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

MEDIA OBJECTIVES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Reach:      % of target exposed at least once
Frequency:  Average number of exposures
Impact:     Qualitative value of exposure
GRPs:       Gross Rating Points (Reach × Frequency)

THE REACH/FREQUENCY TRADE-OFF:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Fixed budget → Choose emphasis

High reach, low frequency:
• New products, awareness building
• Broad campaigns

Low reach, high frequency:
• Complex messages
• Competitive markets
• Reminder campaigns

MEDIA TYPES COMPARISON:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Medium      Pros                 Cons
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Television  Reach, impact,       Cost, DVR
            demonstration        skipping

Digital     Targeting,           Fragmentation,
            measurable           ad blocking

Radio       Cost, local,         Audio only,
            frequency            background

Print       Credibility,         Declining
            permanence           readership

Outdoor     Reach, location,     Limited
            24/7 visibility      message

MEDIA SCHEDULING:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Continuous:   Steady throughout period
Flighting:    Alternating on/off
Pulsing:      Low continuous + periodic bursts
Seasonal:     Heavy during peak periods
```

### Digital Metrics

```
KEY DIGITAL METRICS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ACQUISITION METRICS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Impressions:         Times ad displayed
Reach:               Unique users reached
Clicks:              Times ad clicked
CTR:                 Click-through rate
                     (Clicks / Impressions)
CPC:                 Cost per click
CPM:                 Cost per thousand impressions

ENGAGEMENT METRICS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Time on site:        Average session duration
Pages per session:   Depth of engagement
Bounce rate:         Single-page visits
Social engagement:   Likes, shares, comments
Video completion:    % who watch to end

CONVERSION METRICS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Conversion rate:     Conversions / Visitors
Cost per acquisition: Total cost / Conversions
Lead conversion rate: Leads → Customers
Cart abandonment:    Carts created vs. completed

EFFICIENCY METRICS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ROAS:               Return on ad spend
                    (Revenue / Ad spend)
CAC:                Customer acquisition cost
LTV:CAC ratio:      Lifetime value / CAC
                    (Target: 3:1 or better)

ATTRIBUTION MODELS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Last click:         100% credit to final touchpoint
First click:        100% credit to initial touchpoint
Linear:             Equal credit to all touchpoints
Time decay:         More credit to recent touches
Position-based:     40/20/40 (first/middle/last)
Data-driven:        ML-based attribution
```

## Marketing Measurement

### Marketing ROI

```
MARKETING ROI FRAMEWORK:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

BASIC MARKETING ROI:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ROI = (Revenue from Marketing - Marketing Cost)
      ─────────────────────────────────────────────
                   Marketing Cost

Example:
Marketing spend:        $100,000
Attributed revenue:     $500,000
Marketing ROI:          ($500K - $100K) / $100K = 400%

CHALLENGES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Attribution across touchpoints
• Brand vs. performance split
• Long-term vs. short-term
• Incrementality (what would have happened anyway?)

MARKETING MIX MODELING:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Statistical analysis of:
• Sales vs. marketing inputs
• Controlling for seasonality, trends
• Allocating credit across channels
• Optimizing budget allocation

INCREMENTALITY TESTING:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Holdout groups (no exposure)
• Geo experiments (different markets)
• A/B testing (randomized)
• Measure lift vs. control
```

## Key Takeaways

1. **Integrate communications** - Consistent message across all channels
2. **Match tools to objectives** - Awareness (advertising), action (promotion)
3. **Digital is essential** - But integrate with traditional
4. **Content is king** - Value-driven, not just promotional
5. **Measure rigorously** - Attribution, incrementality, ROI
6. **Adapt continuously** - Digital enables real-time optimization
