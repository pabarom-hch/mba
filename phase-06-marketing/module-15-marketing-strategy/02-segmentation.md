# Market Segmentation

## Learning Objectives

- Understand the purpose and value of segmentation
- Apply different segmentation bases
- Develop actionable customer segments
- Profile and size segments effectively
- Evaluate segment attractiveness

---

## Introduction

Market segmentation divides a heterogeneous market into smaller, more homogeneous groups that respond similarly to marketing efforts. Effective segmentation enables focused marketing, better resource allocation, and stronger competitive positioning. This chapter covers segmentation bases, methods, and evaluation criteria.

---

## Segmentation Fundamentals

### Why Segment?

```
VALUE OF SEGMENTATION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PROBLEM WITHOUT SEGMENTATION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• "Average" customer doesn't exist
• One-size-fits-all marketing is inefficient
• Competitors can pick off underserved niches
• Can't optimize product/price/promotion

BENEFITS OF SEGMENTATION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Better customer understanding
• More relevant marketing messages
• Higher conversion and response rates
• Efficient resource allocation
• Competitive differentiation
• Premium pricing opportunities
• Customer loyalty through fit

SEGMENTATION EVOLUTION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Mass marketing  → Segment marketing → Niche marketing
"One for all"     "One for each"      "One for few"

                    → Micro-marketing → Individual
                      "One for some"    "One for one"
```

### Segmentation Criteria

```
EFFECTIVE SEGMENT REQUIREMENTS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

MEASURABLE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Can you size and profile the segment?
• Demographics available?
• Purchase data accessible?
• Segment membership identifiable?

SUBSTANTIAL:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Is the segment large enough to serve profitably?
• Revenue potential sufficient?
• Worth dedicated marketing effort?
• Covers fixed costs?

ACCESSIBLE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Can you reach the segment effectively?
• Media channels exist?
• Distribution available?
• Sales force can target?

DIFFERENTIABLE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Does the segment respond differently?
• Different needs from other segments?
• Different value proposition needed?
• Worth treating distinctly?

ACTIONABLE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Can you design programs for the segment?
• Clear marketing implications?
• Feasible to serve differently?
• Organization can execute?

SEGMENT EVALUATION CHECKLIST:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
□ Can we measure segment size and characteristics?
□ Is segment large enough to be profitable?
□ Can we reach segment with marketing/distribution?
□ Does segment respond differently than others?
□ Can we design distinct programs for segment?
```

## Segmentation Process

### Step-by-Step Approach

```
SEGMENTATION PROCESS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

STEP 1: SELECT SEGMENTATION BASIS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Choose variables based on:
• Industry norms and available data
• Strategic objectives
• Customer behavior patterns

STEP 2: DEVELOP SEGMENT PROFILES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For each segment, describe:
• Demographics/Firmographics
• Behaviors and preferences
• Needs and pain points
• Size and growth

STEP 3: EVALUATE SEGMENTS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Assess each on:
• Attractiveness (size, growth, profit potential)
• Accessibility (can you reach them?)
• Fit (does it match your capabilities?)

STEP 4: SELECT TARGET SEGMENTS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Choose segments based on:
• Strategic priority
• Resource availability
• Competitive positioning

STEP 5: DEVELOP SEGMENT STRATEGIES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For each target segment:
• Positioning
• Marketing mix (4Ps)
• Metrics and goals
```

### Segmentation Methods

```
QUANTITATIVE SEGMENTATION METHODS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CLUSTER ANALYSIS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Group customers by similarity on multiple variables
• K-means clustering
• Hierarchical clustering
• Choose number of clusters (balance: too few =
  meaningless, too many = unmanageable)

FACTOR ANALYSIS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Reduce many variables to fewer underlying factors
• Identify latent dimensions
• Simplify segmentation scheme

LATENT CLASS ANALYSIS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Find hidden groups based on response patterns
• Probabilistic segment membership
• Each customer has probability of belonging

CONJOINT ANALYSIS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Segment by attribute preferences
• What features matter most?
• Willingness to pay for attributes
• Trade-offs customers make
```

### Segment Sizing

```
SEGMENT SIZING FRAMEWORK:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

STEP 1: ESTIMATE SEGMENT POPULATION
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Use demographic data to estimate:
• Total market population
• % matching segment criteria
• Segment population

STEP 2: ESTIMATE PURCHASE BEHAVIOR
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Purchase incidence (% who buy category)
• Purchase frequency (times per year)
• Average purchase amount

STEP 3: CALCULATE SEGMENT VALUE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Segment population
× Purchase incidence
× Frequency
× Average amount
= Segment market value

STEP 4: ESTIMATE OBTAINABLE SHARE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Segment market value
× Realistic market share
= Addressable opportunity

EXAMPLE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Target metro population:         5,000,000
Match segment criteria:          × 8%
Segment population:              400,000

Category purchase rate:          × 60%
Potential buyers:                240,000

Purchases per year:              × 4
Average purchase:                × $50
Segment value:                   $48,000,000

Obtainable share (10%):          × 10%
Addressable opportunity:         $4,800,000
```

## Key Takeaways

1. **Segment with purpose** - Enable better marketing, not just analysis
2. **Apply multiple bases** - Combine demographics, psychographics, behavior
3. **Validate segment quality** - Measurable, substantial, accessible, differentiable, actionable
4. **Profile segments richly** - Go beyond demographics to motivations
5. **Size segments quantitatively** - Know the revenue opportunity
6. **B2B is nested** - Move from macro to micro segmentation
