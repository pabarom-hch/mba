# Case Study: Coca-Cola's Global Portfolio Strategy

## Executive Summary

Coca-Cola manages one of the world's most complex brand portfolios, with 200+ brands serving billions of consumers across 200+ countries. This case examines how Coca-Cola segments markets, positions brands, and manages its portfolio to maximize growth while protecting its iconic flagship brand.

**Key Question:** How does Coca-Cola use segmentation and portfolio strategy to address changing consumer preferences while maintaining the world's most valuable beverage brand?

```

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

GROWTH ENGINE BRANDS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Various local/regional brands

Strategy: Invest or exit

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Sparkling  60%      70%        50%

Juice      12%      10%        15%

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

### The Coca-Cola Zero Success Story

```
COCA-COLA ZERO CASE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

THE PROBLEM (Pre-2005):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Diet Coke positioned for women
• Men wouldn't drink "diet" products
• Health-conscious men not served
• Missed segment opportunity

THE SOLUTION:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Launch Coca-Cola Zero (2005)
• Position: "Real Coca-Cola taste, zero sugar"
• Target: Men who want taste, not calories
• Black packaging (masculine vs. Diet silver)

REPOSITIONING (2021):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Renamed "Coca-Cola Zero Sugar"
• Reformulated for closer taste to Classic
• New packaging, marketing campaign
• Broader target (not just men)

RESULTS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Coca-Cola Zero growth:
2018: +6%
2019: +9%
2020: +4%
2021: +10%
2022: +11%

Now ~10% of Coca-Cola volume
Highest growth variant
Cannibalization of Diet Coke, but overall gain
```

## Exhibit 3: Global vs. Local Strategy

### Glocalization Approach

```
COCA-COLA'S GLOCAL FRAMEWORK:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

GLOBAL ELEMENTS (Standardized):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Coca-Cola brand and trademark
• Core brand positioning
• Quality standards
• Innovation platforms
• Major campaigns (Olympics, World Cup)

LOCAL ELEMENTS (Adapted):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Product portfolio mix
• Flavors and variants
• Pricing strategy
• Distribution approach
• Local marketing execution
• Local brand acquisitions

EXAMPLE - JAPAN:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Global:     Coca-Cola brand present
Local:      Georgia Coffee (top seller)
            Ayataka Green Tea
            Aquarius Sports
            Lemon-do Lemon Sour
            50+ Japan-only brands

Japan is Coca-Cola's third-largest market
Coke Classic is minor; coffee/tea dominate
```

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ASIA-PACIFIC:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

AFRICA:

## Teaching Note

### Learning Objectives

This case teaches students to:
- Apply segmentation at a global scale
- Manage complex brand portfolios
- Balance global efficiency with local relevance
- Evolve iconic brands without destroying equity

### Key Insights

**Coca-Cola's strategic genius:**
1. Portfolio approach protects against any single trend
2. Brand architecture allows targeted positioning
3. Standalone brands enable credibility in wellness
4. Glocalization maximizes relevance and efficiency
5. Zero-sugar variants capture health-conscious without cannibalizing

**Strategic tensions:**
1. Core brand generates profit but faces headwinds
2. Diversification creates complexity
3. Local adaptation is expensive
4. Youth relevance may conflict with heritage

### Recommended Position

**Portfolio Strategy:**
- Maintain investment in Coca-Cola Classic (cash cow)
- Accelerate Coca-Cola Zero Sugar (growth engine)
- Build coffee/tea for new occasions
- Prune underperforming local brands
- Acquire strategically in high-growth categories

**Flagship Evolution:**
- Don't abandon Coca-Cola Classic
- Evolve through Zero Sugar cannibalization
- Smaller sizes (reduce sugar per serving)
- Position on joy/experience, not refreshment
- Limited editions for excitement without core change

**Global/Local Framework:**
- Brand positioning: Global (Coca-Cola = happiness)
- Portfolio mix: Local (what categories matter)
- Marketing execution: Local (cultural relevance)
- Innovation: Global platforms, local variants

### Discussion Questions

1. Is "total beverage company" the right strategy, or should Coca-Cola focus on its core?
2. Should Coca-Cola ever abandon the full-sugar flagship?
3. How should Coca-Cola compete with energy drinks for young consumers?
4. What would you do if you ran Coca-Cola's marketing?
